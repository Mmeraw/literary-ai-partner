Policy

Clear limits preserve trust.

RevisionGrade should feel governed, not slippery. The page should make the rules legible enough that serious users feel protected and abusers understand there is no soft path through the system.

Free access is narrow by design

Free Starter includes up to 3,000 words for excerpt-level evaluation. It demonstrates the framework without handing away the manuscript-grade product.

WAVE stays manuscript-grade

WAVE analysis is available only for manuscripts of 25,000 or more words submitted as a single work. Separate excerpts do not accumulate into manuscript-grade analysis.

Pause, don’t punish

When a monthly allowance is reached, new analyses pause. Previous results remain available, and analysis resumes at reset or upgrade.

Work-tied outputs, not templates

Query letters, pitches, synopses, comparables, and adaptation materials are generated from the evaluated manuscript record, not generic prompting detached from the work.

Pricing notes

Questions serious writers will ask immediately.

The pricing page should answer objections with precision. It should not bury the rules in support articles or vague “learn more” links.

Is RevisionGrade priced per word?

No. Plans are metered by total words analyzed within a monthly allowance. Users are not charged line by line or pass by pass, and all prior results remain accessible when an allowance is reached.

What counts toward manuscript capacity?

Every evaluation, revision cycle, and work-tied output draws from the total words analyzed within the plan. The customer-facing rule stays clear even though backend costs come from multiple systems.

Why not give away the full evaluation or repair path?

Evaluation can prove seriousness. REVISE retains the premium corrective sequence, chapter route, and dependency-aware logic that turns diagnosis into actual manuscript repair.

Can multiple excerpts unlock WAVE?

No. Manuscript-grade WAVE analysis requires a single manuscript submission of at least 25,000 words. Fragments do not stack into premium eligibility.
