# WAVE-31-LW: Chapter-Ending Pressure Continuity (Literary Dense Mode)

**Canon ID:** WAVE-31-LW  
**Status:** FROZEN  
**Type:** Execution Canon (Wave Variant)  
**Authority:** Mike Meraw  
**Frozen Date:** 2026-05-28  
**Destination:** Volume I - Wave Canon (Tsunami 3 / Structural Integrity), cross-ref Volume II (STRUCTURE, TENSION), Volume II-A, Volume III, Volume IV

## Purpose

Constrain Wave 31 so that for literary-dense, atmosphere-driven manuscripts, chapter endings preserve pressure continuity rather than defaulting to neat resolution or artificial cliffhangers.

## Scope

Triggered when:
- Manuscript is tagged `mode = "literary_dense"` or equivalent
- Volume I Wave 31 ("Chapter-Ending Integrity") is active

Applies to:
- Chapter-ending evaluation after Waves 1-30 structural checks are satisfied
- End-of-chapter pressure assessment in literary horror and dense narrative modes

## Core Doctrine

1. **Pressure must land, not resolve.** A chapter ending in literary-dense mode should leave the reader carrying unresolved weight into the next chapter.
2. **No artificial cliffhangers.** Manufactured suspense is not pressure. Earned dread, unresolved tension, and open threat are pressure.
3. **Compression at chapter endings must not flatten atmosphere.** The last paragraph of a chapter is not a cleanup zone.
4. **Quiet endings are valid when they increase dread.** Silence after violence, stillness after chaos, calm after threat - these are pressure vectors, not resolution.

## Protected Elements (31-LW-P)

These elements are presumptively protected at chapter endings:
- Final sensory images that create lingering unease
- Ritual fragment closers (incomplete prayers, broken cadences)
- Environmental pressure signals (weather, sound, absence of sound)
- Symbolic object placement in final position
- Unresolved dialogue that carries threat forward

## Valid Compression Targets (31-LW-T)

Wave 31-LW may target at chapter endings:
- Explanatory wrap-up sentences that discharge tension
- Redundant emotional labeling after the scene already showed it
- Bridge phrases that narrate the transition instead of letting the ending breathe
- Summary statements that restate what the chapter already demonstrated

## Forbidden Actions (31-LW-F)

Wave 31-LW must NOT:
- Add resolution where the author left deliberate openness
- Smooth a jagged ending into a clean one
- Remove the final sensory beat to "tighten" the chapter
- Replace atmospheric pressure with plot-summary closure

## Pass/Fail Rule

A revision pass is non-compliant with WAVE-31-LW if it:
- Removes chapter-ending pressure in favor of neatness
- Adds explanatory closure the author deliberately omitted
- Breaks a ritual or atmospheric closer for compression
- Produces an ending that is cleaner but carries less weight into the next chapter
