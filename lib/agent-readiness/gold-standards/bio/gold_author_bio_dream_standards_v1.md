# Gold Author Bio DREAM Standards v1

## Purpose

This artifact defines DREAM/GOLD author biography exemplars for RevisionGrade Agent Readiness.

These are not generic author-bio samples. They calibrate the generator toward agent-facing biographies that are:

- fact-governed
- manuscript-specific
- professionally restrained
- authority-forward without résumé dumping
- connected to the author's lived or professional credibility for the manuscript

## Runtime rule

Use these examples for structure, emphasis, tone, and quality calibration only. Never copy manuscript titles, personal facts, credentials, residences, project names, or publication claims into a generated biography unless those facts are present in the selected author's supplied profile materials or verified project metadata.

## DREAM principle

A DREAM-level author bio answers one question quickly:

```txt
Why is this author credible to write this specific manuscript?
```

The best bio does not list every credential. It selects the credentials that matter for the manuscript being submitted.

# Exemplar 1 — Query Letter Bio / Cartel Babies

```txt
artifact_type: gold_standard_author_bio
id: gold_author_bio_cartel_babies_query_v1
tier: dream_gold
category: query_letter_bio
best_for: upmarket suspense, literary suspense, cartel-system narrative, regional/lived-experience authority
target_length: 90-130 words
```

## Exemplar

Michael J. Meraw is a former Canadian Armed Forces pilot and aerospace executive turned author. He writes fiction that explores survival, consequence, and the forces that shape both natural and human worlds. Splitting his time between Canada and Mexico, including living in Culiacán, Sinaloa, he draws on direct regional experience to inform *Cartel Babies*, a character-driven literary suspense novel set inside the hidden labor systems of the cartel economy. His broader work spans eco-horror and speculative fiction within the EᴠØ-Myth Universe™, including *Let the River Decide*, *Dominatus I: The Lost World of Mythoamphibia*, and *Return to the Source*. Before turning to fiction, he founded a startup and pitched to producers on CBC's *Dragons' Den*.

## Why this is DREAM/GOLD

- Leads with relevant authority: military pilot + aerospace executive.
- Connects directly to the manuscript: Culiacán/Sinaloa + cartel labor systems.
- Establishes author brand without over-explaining.
- Mentions related works as brand coherence, not résumé padding.
- Includes one memorable proof point: CBC's *Dragons' Den*.
- Avoids age-signaling language where possible.

## Caution

This version is strongest when the query letter has room for a robust bio. For stricter forms, use the QueryTracker/short bio exemplar.

# Exemplar 2 — Professional Agent Bio / Cartel Babies

```txt
artifact_type: gold_standard_author_bio
id: gold_author_bio_cartel_babies_agent_submission_v1
tier: dream_gold
category: agent_submission_bio
best_for: agency forms, submission package bio sheet, longer query forms, author profile panels
target_length: 130-190 words
```

## Exemplar

Michael J. Meraw (Major, Ret.) is a former Canadian Armed Forces pilot and aerospace leader whose fiction explores survival, consequence, and the systems—both natural and human—that shape our world. Splitting his time between British Columbia and Culiacán, Sinaloa, he draws on lived regional experience to inform *Cartel Babies*, an upmarket literary suspense novel set within the hidden labor engines of the synthetic-drug economy.

Beyond his work in suspense, Michael is the creator of the EᴠØ-Myth Universe™, a transmedia project spanning eco-horror and speculative mythology. His titles include *Let the River Decide*, *The Lost World of Mythoamphibia*, and the prehistoric novella *Return to the Source*. A graduate of Stanford's SCPM program and an MBA, he previously founded a startup and pitched to producers on CBC's *Dragons' Den*. He currently lives and writes in the shadow of the Sierra Madre.

## Why this is DREAM/GOLD

- Best for a fuller Agent Readiness package, not a tight 450-word query.
- Uses rank and credentials where authority helps positioning.
- Connects location directly to manuscript authority.
- Ends with literary branding rather than résumé clutter.
- Balances commercial credibility, literary identity, and manuscript-specific authority.

## Caution

For a compact query letter, this may be too long. Use when the bio is a standalone submission-package section or when an agency form explicitly asks for a longer biography.

This is not manuscript-specific enough for a full query-letter bio unless paired with a query body that already establishes the author's connection to the manuscript.

# Exemplar 4 — Website / Press Bio

```txt
artifact_type: gold_standard_author_bio
id: gold_author_bio_website_press_v1
tier: gold
category: website_or_press_bio
best_for: author website, media kit, book page, press materials
target_length: 250-450 words
```

## Exemplar

Michael J. Meraw is an author whose work explores the dark intersection of environmental collapse, existential reckoning, and humanity's unrelenting impact on the planet. A retired Canadian Armed Forces pilot and former aerospace leader, his perspective on survival, control, and power dynamics shapes his exploration of humanity's tenuous hold on the natural world.

He channels these experiences into eco-horror, suspense, and speculative narratives that challenge the myth of human dominance. His stories trace what happens when the earth stops forgiving us—when rivers remember, forests reclaim, species speak back, and human-made systems become closed ecosystems of inherited violence.

His projects include *Cartel Babies*, an upmarket literary suspense novel set in contemporary Sinaloa; *Let the River Decide*, a literary wilderness novel about environmental collapse in northern Canada; *The Lost World of Mythoamphibia*, a literary eco-horror novel and screenplay; and *Return to the Source*, a prehistoric novella about the last Neanderthal.

Across novels, screenplays, and transmedia projects, Meraw's writing examines survival, consequence, and the forces—natural and human—that shape civilization.

## Why this is GOLD

- Strong for branding and website use.
- Too expansive for most query letters.
- Builds thematic identity across projects.
- Gives the author a coherent professional/literary frame.

## Caution

Do not use website-bio style in a query letter unless an agent explicitly asks for a long author statement. Query bios must be shorter, more direct, and more manuscript-specific.

## Verified author fact categories from supplied materials

The generator may use these kinds of facts only when present in the selected author's supplied materials:

- Former Canadian Armed Forces pilot.
- Served two decades in military aviation and training.
- Rose to the rank of Major.
- Completed BComm, AGDM, and MBA.
- Held senior roles at Pratt & Whitney Canada.
- Earned Stanford Certified Project Manager / SCPM credential where supplied.
- Founded Technoslips Inc.
- Pitched on CBC's *Dragons' Den*.
- Sold Technoslips in 2019 where supplied.
- Writes from / splits time between Canada, British Columbia, Mexico, and/or Culiacán when supplied.
- Writes literary suspense, eco-thrillers, eco-horror, speculative fiction, and related literary projects where project metadata supports it.
- Project titles may include *Cartel Babies*, *Let the River Decide*, *The Lost World of Mythoamphibia*, and *Return to the Source* when supplied.

## Forbidden unless explicitly supplied for the selected author

- Awards.
- Publications.
- Agent representation.
- Bestseller claims.
- Film/TV deals.
- Degrees or certifications not supplied.
- Military decorations or tours not supplied in the active author profile.
- Residence claims not supplied in the active author profile.
- Identity claims unless the author explicitly opts in for that submission.

# Runtime selection guidance

## Use `query_letter_bio` when

- The bio is embedded inside a query letter.
- The query has a hard cap around 350-450 words.
- The bio must close the letter cleanly.

## Use `agent_submission_bio` when

- The package includes a standalone author bio.
- The agency form asks for author biography.
- The author wants stronger professional positioning.

## Use `short_form_submission_bio` when

- The field is short.
- QueryTracker or similar forms impose length limits.
- The author wants low-age-signal phrasing.

## Use `website_or_press_bio` when

- The output is for author website, press kit, book page, or media packet.
- Branding matters more than query compression.

---

# Primary rule for Agent Readiness

Prefer manuscript-relevant authority over generic literary credentials.

A debut author with lived regional experience, professional expertise, or subject-matter proximity may have a stronger agent bio than a writer with generic publication language but no connection to the manuscript.
