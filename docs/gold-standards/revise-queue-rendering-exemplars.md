# Revise Queue Rendering Exemplars

## Purpose

This document provides gold-standard Revise Queue examples for Phase 0 warmup, prompt grounding, validation tests, and UI regression testing.

These examples define the desired output shape.

---

# Golden Rule

> The opening diagnostic says what is wrong.  
> A/B/C show what the manuscript could become.

---

# Exemplar 1 — Chapter 1 / Twillow Says “Move Aside, Small Fry”

## Queue Metadata

```yaml
priority: should
criterion: Narrative Drive & Momentum
operation: insert_after_selected_passage
readiness: ready_for_revise
chapter: Chapter 1
```

## Issue Statement

Newton’s first response to Twillow needs a visible choice beat before the attack escalates.

## Diagnostic Brief

### Symptom

The scene jumps from Twillow’s threat to Newton being hit before Newton makes an active choice.

### Cause

Newton’s internal and physical response is summarized too quickly, so the reader does not see him choose whether to fold, freeze, flee, or fight.

### Fix Strategy

Insert a short embodied decision beat after Twillow’s command and before the first strike.

### Reader Impact

The reader understands Newton’s fear, courage, and agency sooner, making the attack feel more consequential.

### Evidence

```text
“Move aside, Small Fry. We spotted those slugs first!”
```

### Operation / Targeting

Insert new prose immediately after Twillow’s line.

### Mistake-Proofing

Do not make Newton sound verbally confident. His agency should be physical and frightened, not heroic speechmaking.

---

## A — Recommended Repair

```text
Newton held the morsel between his teeth and went still. The muck gripped both feet, but that was not the only reason he stayed. Twillow’s shadow covered the slugs, then Newton’s forelegs, then his throat.

He swallowed hard, set one sore foot deeper into the mud, and made himself remain between Twillow and the food.
```

## B — Rhythm Variant

```text
Newton froze with the food still in his mouth. The smart thing was to spit it out, lower his head, and let Twillow pass.

Instead, he swallowed.

Then he stayed exactly where he was.
```

## C — Bolder Rendering Shift

```text
Newton’s jaws stopped working. The slug went soft and tasteless in his mouth as Twillow filled the world in front of him. Every lesson his mother had ever hummed into him said move, live, forgive.

His feet sank deeper.

Not this time.
```

# Exemplar 2 — Chapter 1 / Repeated Predator Comparison

## Queue Metadata

```yaml
priority: must
criterion: Prose Control & Line-Level Craft
operation: replace_selected_passage
readiness: ready_for_revise
chapter: Chapter 1
```

## Issue Statement

The same hobo spider / wolf spider comparison repeats too soon and weakens the second escalation.

## Diagnostic Brief

### Symptom

The scene uses the same comparison before and after Twillow’s first attack.

### Cause

The repeated image asks one metaphor to do the same work twice, so the second beat feels copied instead of escalated.

### Fix Strategy

Replace the second comparison with fresh prose that shows Newton’s warning display failing against Twillow.

### Reader Impact

The reader feels the second moment as a new humiliation rather than a repeated description.

### Evidence

```text
Twillow was double his age and one-third larger, so it was like watching a gentle hobo spider paired up against an aggressive wolf spider.
```

### Operation / Targeting

Replace the second occurrence of the repeated comparison after Newton flashes his orangish-red belly poisons.

### Mistake-Proofing

Keep the animal-world frame and the fable voice. Do not flatten the image into generic realism.

---

## A — Recommended Repair

```text
Newton instinctively raised his head and tail and flashed his orangish-red belly poisons. The display was supposed to warn predators away. Against Twillow, it only made him look smaller.
```

## B — Rhythm Variant

```text
Newton raised his head and tail, flashing the orangish-red warning of his belly poisons. It was the only threat his body had left. Twillow laughed at it.
```

## C — Bolder Rendering Shift

```text
Newton arched until his orangish-red belly poisons shone through the dew. The warning colours trembled beneath him, bright as a prayer and just as easily ignored.
```

# Exemplar 3 — Chapter 2 / Rana Chooses Mercy

## Queue Metadata

```yaml
priority: should
criterion: Character Agency
operation: expand_beat
readiness: ready_for_revise
chapter: Chapter 2
```

## Issue Statement

Rana’s rescue choice is strong, but the moment can carry more moral weight before she acts.

## Diagnostic Brief

### Symptom

Rana moves quickly from discovering the injured newt to treating him.

### Cause

The scene establishes that newts are enemies, but Rana’s internal crossing of that boundary happens too fast.

### Fix Strategy

Add a short moral hesitation beat before she presses the wound.

### Reader Impact

The reader sees Rana choose mercy rather than merely perform medical action.

### Evidence

```text
Instinctively, Rana applied pressure to the tail to slow the bleeding.
```

### Operation / Targeting

Insert or replace a short beat immediately before Rana applies pressure.

### Mistake-Proofing

Do not turn Rana into a human ethicist. Keep the choice sensory, immediate, and amphibian-grounded.

---

## A — Recommended Repair

```text
Rana lowered her face to the bleeding stump and smelled enemy, fear, and the thin metallic tang of a life leaking away.

One bite would not help her. One hop away would not shame her. A newt was a newt.

But the creature’s forefoot twitched, small and helpless against the mud.

Rana pressed both hands to the wound.
```

## B — Rhythm Variant

```text
She knew what he was. Enemy. Poison-skin. Egg-eater. A rough-skinned curse with legs.

Then he made a sound no enemy had ever made in her hearing.

It was not a threat.

It was a plea.
```

## C — Bolder Rendering Shift

```text
Rana stared at the torn place where his tail had been and felt the old laws crowd around her: hate him, leave him, let the swamp decide.

Instead, she broke a reed with her teeth and wrapped it tight around the wound.
```

# Exemplar 4 — Chapter 4 / Lacerta’s Bracelet Reveal

## Queue Metadata

```yaml
priority: must
criterion: Emotional Consequence
operation: insert_after_selected_passage
readiness: ready_for_revise
chapter: Chapter 4
```

## Issue Statement

Newton discovering his mother’s bracelet is a major emotional turn and needs an immediate grief beat before he leaves.

## Diagnostic Brief

### Symptom

The bracelet reveal is followed too quickly by Newton departing for home.

### Cause

The scene gives the plot fact of Lacerta’s death but does not give Newton enough embodied reaction time.

### Fix Strategy

Insert a contained grief beat immediately after the bracelet reveal.

### Reader Impact

The reader absorbs Lacerta’s death through Newton’s body and memory, not only through information.

### Evidence

```text
On top, they saw Lacerta’s bracelet.
```

### Operation / Targeting

Insert new prose immediately after the bracelet reveal.

### Mistake-Proofing

Do not over-explain Lacerta’s death. The bracelet should carry the emotional recognition.

---

## A — Recommended Repair

```text
Newton did not touch it at first. The little ring of leaf fibre, tree sap, and baby teeth lay slick among the turtle’s leavings, too familiar to belong there.

His forelegs buckled.

Rana reached toward him, then stopped herself. Some griefs had teeth.
```

## B — Rhythm Variant

```text
Newton knew the bracelet before his mind agreed to know it. His baby teeth. His mother’s scent. The careful knot only Lacerta tied that way.

The lake noises fell away.

There was only the bracelet, and the space where his mother no longer was.
```

## C — Bolder Rendering Shift

```text
The bracelet stared back at him from the mess as if Lacerta herself had opened one silver eye. Newton backed away, shaking his head, but the truth followed him step for step.
```

# Exemplar 5 — Chapter 5 / Thorander Lore Compression

## Queue Metadata

```yaml
priority: should
criterion: Pacing & Exposition Management
operation: split_and_compress
readiness: ready_for_revise
chapter: Chapter 5
```

## Issue Statement

Thorander’s origin lore is central, but the explanation arrives as a long lecture and needs more dramatic exchange.

## Diagnostic Brief

### Symptom

The father-daughter scene spends a long stretch in uninterrupted myth explanation.

### Cause

The lore carries important worldbuilding, but Rana’s present-tense pressure and challenge are underused during the exposition.

### Fix Strategy

Break the lore into a sharper exchange where Rana questions the inherited hatred.

### Reader Impact

The reader receives the same thematic information with more tension, character friction, and forward movement.

### Evidence

```text
“So, what does this mythoamphibia teach us about life?”
```

### Operation / Targeting

Compress the explanatory passage into a dramatic father-daughter exchange.

### Mistake-Proofing

Do not remove the mythic register. The fix should reduce lecture density without erasing lore importance.

---

## A — Recommended Repair

```text
“So Prince Urodela attacked his own brother’s young?” Rana asked.

Thorander’s throat pulsed once. “He attacked the future that threatened him.”

“And we kept the hatred alive because he was afraid?”

“Because fear survives when repeated by elders.”

Rana looked toward the water, where everything fed and fled in turn. “Then maybe elders can repeat something else.”
```

## B — Rhythm Variant

```text
“Father, if Anura and Urodela were brothers, then frogs and newts are not strangers.”

“Blood does not prevent war.”

“No. But it makes the war uglier.”

Thorander did not answer. For the first time, the old story seemed heavier in his mouth than in hers.
```

## C — Bolder Rendering Shift

```text
“You call it froglore,” Rana said, “but it sounds like a wound we keep licking.”

Thorander’s eyes narrowed.

She bowed her head, but not her thought. “If Gorf punished the brothers for failing love, why do we honour them by failing it again?”
```

# Phase 0 Usage

Phase 0 should load these exemplars before evaluation and derive these rules:

1. The diagnostic explains.
2. A/B/C enact.
3. Evidence must be author-facing.
4. Operation must be executable.
5. Candidate prose must be manuscript-ready.
6. Rationale is secondary and never inside the prose block.
7. Bad candidates are routed to Needs Targeting, not shown as ready.

---

# UI Regression Assertions

For every exemplar:

- Header contains issue statement.
- Diagnostic brief contains symptom, cause, fix strategy, reader impact, evidence, operation.
- A/B/C prose blocks contain only candidate prose.
- A/B/C labels use the stable roles.
- Candidate prose does not repeat the issue statement.
- Candidate prose does not contain meta-editorial language.
- Rationale, if present, appears outside the prose block.
