# Return to the Source — Revise Gold Standard Fixture

Status: gold-standard Revise fixture v1  
Benchmark family: cosmological-origin fiction / species conflict / divine-family governance / Aeon multiverse architecture  
Runtime role: calibration fixture for six-part Revise diagnostics and A/B/C repair candidates.

## Fixture principle

A Return to the Source Revise card must protect the manuscript's flagship scale: Neanderthal/Homo sapiens contact, bone-memory law, tribal governance, extinction pressure, love across boundaries, God/Anima/Sisters divine-family tension, Aeon-level multiverse design, imperfection, free will, and creation recursion. It must never flatten the work into generic prehistory, generic theology, or generic fantasy cosmology.

## Gold-standard card example

### Card title

Make the bone-memory law beat carry both survival knowledge and moral cost.

### Source evidence

Use manuscript-local evidence around the law-bone, bone teaching, ancestor memory, tribal command, ridge law, or a scene where survival knowledge becomes governance. The evaluator must cite the exact passage available in the run; this fixture supplies card shape, not replacement evidence.

### Six-part diagnostic

| Part | Gold-standard content |
|---|---|
| Evidence | Anchor to the exact passage where bone, law, memory, teaching, or tribal authority is embodied in object or ritual. |
| Symptom | The beat may establish law or survival knowledge but not fully reveal the moral cost of preserving that knowledge through violence, obedience, or erasure. |
| Cause | The scene may carry multiple layers—artifact, teaching, authority, ancestor memory, species pressure—but only one is explicit on the page. |
| Fix direction | Add a compact recognition beat showing what the law protects and what it damages. Keep the repair embodied in bone, hand, voice, terrain, or child response. |
| Reader effect | The reader feels the law-bone as civilization infrastructure and moral burden, not as decorative prehistoric texture. |
| Mistake-proofing | Do not modernize the character psychology. Do not explain the theme abstractly. Do not turn tribal law into contemporary essay language. |

### Repair goal

Make bone-memory law legible as survival archive, governance mechanism, and moral wound in a scene-level repair.

## Candidate repairs

### Candidate A — Conservative repair

Add one embodied sentence after the teaching beat: a hand tightening on bone, a child flinching, an elder pausing, or a silence that reveals law as burden. The sentence should imply moral cost without naming it abstractly.

### Candidate B — Balanced repair

Add a short paragraph that contrasts what the law-bone preserves with what it demands. The repair should remain in prehistoric sensory register—bone, ash, ridge, breath, blood, or fire—not modern reflection.

### Candidate C — Bold repair

Seed the law-bone earlier as a survival tool, then pay it off later as a moral test. The same object should first teach survival, then expose the cost of survival when applied to another human lineage, child, captive, or beloved figure.

## Acceptance criteria

A repaired passage passes if:

- bone/law/memory remains concrete and embodied;
- the reader understands both survival value and moral cost;
- the passage does not become modern thesis language;
- Neanderthal/Homo sapiens pressure remains active;
- tribal governance is clarified without exposition dumping;
- the repair preserves the novel's mythic-prehistoric register.

## Risk notes

- Do not make prehistoric characters sound like modern ethicists.
- Do not reduce the bone to symbol only; it is also archive and authority.
- Do not over-explain divine/cosmological implications in the early tribal layer.
- Do not contradict accepted species-contact or ancestor-memory architecture.

## Withhold examples

Withhold a card if it says only:

- "Clarify the symbolism of the bone."
- "Add more theme."
- "Explain the tribal law."
- "Make the Neanderthal conflict clearer."

Those are not specific enough for Workbench release.

## What this teaches Revise

This fixture teaches Revise to generate repairs for flagship-scale literary architecture while staying scene-bound, embodied, manuscript-grounded, and canon-safe.
