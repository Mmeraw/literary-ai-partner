# Cartel Babies — Revise Gold Standard Fixture

Status: gold-standard Revise fixture v1  
Benchmark family: criminal network suspense / captivity / identity transformation  
Runtime role: calibration fixture for six-part Revise diagnostics and A/B/C repair candidates.

## Fixture principle

A Cartel Babies Revise card must preserve cartel-system pressure, captivity logic, identity transformation, family stakes, and object-symbol continuity. It must never flatten the manuscript into generic cartel danger or generic action-thriller advice.

## Gold-standard card example

### Card title

Clarify Paolito-to-Paul identity transformation without overexplaining the rename.

### Source evidence

Use manuscript-local evidence around the Paolito / Paul transition, embassy / protected-identity material, and Michael / Benjamin aftercare chain. The evaluator must cite the exact passage available in the run; this fixture supplies the card shape, not replacement evidence.

### Six-part diagnostic

| Part | Gold-standard content |
|---|---|
| Evidence | Anchor the card to the exact scene where Paolito’s identity shifts toward Paul or where the protected-identity pathway is formalized. |
| Symptom | The identity transition carries major emotional and legal weight, but the reader may not receive enough staged recognition of what changes internally, relationally, and institutionally. |
| Cause | The manuscript may treat the rename / identity shift as plot logistics instead of a three-layer transformation: child, family, and state protection. |
| Fix direction | Add a short beat that distinguishes what Paolito loses, what Paul permits him to survive, and what Michael / Benjamin must accept about that transformation. |
| Reader effect | The reader feels the cost of survival rather than reading the new identity as administrative cleanup. |
| Mistake-proofing | Do not explain the entire legal pathway. Do not add melodrama. Do not weaken cartel threat by making the transition feel easy or complete. |

### Repair goal

Make the identity transition emotionally legible, legally credible, and canon-safe in one compact scene beat.

## Candidate repairs

### Candidate A — Conservative repair

Add one paragraph immediately after the identity/protection material in which Michael notices the difference between calling the boy Paolito and hearing the official record call him Paul. Keep it observational, restrained, and grounded in the paperwork, the room, and the boy’s physical response.

### Candidate B — Balanced repair

Add a two-beat exchange between Michael and Benjamin or Michael and the official handling the transition. The exchange should name the practical necessity of Paul while allowing Michael to privately register that Paolito is not simply gone. This gives the reader legal clarity and emotional residue without exposition.

### Candidate C — Bold repair

Seed the Paolito-to-Paul transition earlier with a small object, phrase, or recurring response, then pay it off at the protection moment. The new identity should feel like both rescue and amputation: the old name remains memory; the new name becomes survival infrastructure.

## Acceptance criteria

A repaired passage passes if:

- the reader can state why Paul exists as a protected identity;
- the reader can feel what Paolito loses;
- Michael / Benjamin’s relationship to the boy is not simplified;
- cartel threat remains active in the background;
- the repair does not create new legal, timeline, or geography contradictions;
- the repair is shorter than the scene it clarifies.

## Risk notes

- Do not turn this into an immigration/legal exposition dump.
- Do not imply witness protection is emotionally complete.
- Do not erase Paolito’s agency or trauma.
- Do not contradict accepted Story Ledger identity authority.

## Withhold examples

Withhold a card if it says only:

- "Clarify the identity change."
- "Make Paolito more emotional."
- "Explain witness protection."
- "Add more backstory."

Those are generic directives, not Revise-ready cards.

## What this teaches Revise

This fixture teaches Revise to protect identity continuity, family cost, and institutional survival mechanics while generating concrete, bounded repair options.
