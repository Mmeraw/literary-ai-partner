# Let the River Decide — Revise Gold Standard Fixture

Status: gold-standard Revise fixture v1  
Benchmark family: expedition / wilderness / environmental witness / leadership under uncertainty  
Runtime role: calibration fixture for six-part Revise diagnostics and A/B/C repair candidates.

## Fixture principle

A Let the River Decide Revise card must preserve expedition credibility, wilderness pressure, environmental witness, cultural protocol, and leadership uncertainty. It must not treat the manuscript as generic outdoor adventure or generic eco-thriller.

## Gold-standard card example

### Card title

Convert environmental observation into escalating expedition pressure.

### Source evidence

Use manuscript-local evidence around a waterway, wildlife absence, damaged ecosystem, contaminated terrain, weather shift, or navigation decision. The evaluator must cite the exact passage available in the run; this fixture supplies card shape, not replacement evidence.

### Six-part diagnostic

| Part | Gold-standard content |
|---|---|
| Evidence | Anchor to the exact observed environmental detail: water quality, wildlife absence, industrial scar, navigation hazard, or silence where life should be present. |
| Symptom | The observation is evocative but may remain descriptive rather than changing the expedition’s risk, decision path, or moral pressure. |
| Cause | The prose may separate witness description from consequence; the river shows damage, but the scene does not yet force a choice. |
| Fix direction | Add a consequence beat: altered route, delayed decision, changed group mood, dog reaction, equipment concern, cultural protocol hesitation, or leadership uncertainty. |
| Reader effect | The reader understands that environmental evidence is not scenic backdrop; it is plot pressure and moral testimony. |
| Mistake-proofing | Do not lecture. Do not add generalized climate commentary. Do not replace sensory evidence with abstract advocacy. |

### Repair goal

Make one environmental observation alter the expedition’s tactical, emotional, or moral trajectory.

## Candidate repairs

### Candidate A — Conservative repair

Add two sentences after the environmental observation showing a concrete expedition consequence: slower paddling, altered landing choice, hesitation to drink/filter water, silence in the group, or the dogs reacting to what the humans cannot yet name.

### Candidate B — Balanced repair

Add a short paragraph that links the observation to leadership uncertainty. Mike or Cliff should register not only what is seen, but what it means for the next decision. The repair should keep the scene in motion and avoid essay language.

### Candidate C — Bold repair

Seed the environmental absence earlier, then pay it off when the expedition reaches the damaged waterway. Let a repeated sensory cue—silence, smell, color, animal absence, or equipment residue—become a structural marker of accumulating threat.

## Acceptance criteria

A repaired passage passes if:

- the environmental detail remains concrete and sensory;
- the expedition’s next decision is affected;
- the scene avoids generic advocacy language;
- leadership uncertainty becomes clearer;
- the wilderness remains active pressure, not scenery;
- the repair preserves memoir-witness credibility where applicable.

## Risk notes

- Do not turn the scene into an environmental lecture.
- Do not overstate scientific certainty beyond the manuscript’s evidence.
- Do not erase cultural protocol or local uncertainty.
- Do not create tactical impossibilities in the route.

## Scores

```yaml
effort_score: low_to_medium

```

## Withhold examples

Withhold a card if it says only:

- "Add more environmental stakes."
- "Make the river more symbolic."
- "Increase tension."
- "Show climate change more clearly."

Those are not sufficiently anchored or actionable.

## What this teaches Revise

This fixture teaches Revise to transform observation into consequence while protecting expedition credibility, witness discipline, and environmental pressure.
