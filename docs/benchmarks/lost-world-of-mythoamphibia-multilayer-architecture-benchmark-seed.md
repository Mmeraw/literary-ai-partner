## Purpose

This benchmark seed teaches RevisionGrade how to evaluate a complex mythic eco-fantasy involving frogs, humans, species boundary pressure, ecological world logic, transformation, mythic taxonomy, interspecies meaning, and morally charged contact between human and amphibian civilizations.

Core calibration problem:

> The manuscript is not merely a fantasy adventure with frogs. It is a multi-species mythic ecosystem where frogs, humans, habitat, metamorphosis, language, myth, and ecological memory form the narrative architecture.

## Layer 12 — Species Boundary Architecture

Track how the manuscript distinguishes and connects humans, frogs, amphibian beings, hybrid mythic figures, and ecological intelligences.

| Boundary type | Evaluation requirement |
|---|---|
| Biological boundary | What is physically frog, human, amphibian, transformed, or mythic? |
| Social boundary | Which groups recognize each other as persons, animals, threats, kin, gods, or outsiders? |
| Moral boundary | Where does empathy cross species lines, and where does it fail? |
| Language boundary | Who can communicate, translate, mishear, or remain unknowable? |
| Ecological boundary | Which habitats permit or forbid contact? |

Calibration rule:

> Species difference is not decoration. It is narrative law.

Failure condition: an evaluation fails if it treats frogs only as cute, comic, symbolic, or monster-like without tracking the governing species boundary system.

---

## Layer 13 — Amphibian Metamorphosis Layer

Amphibian life cycles create a natural symbolic grammar: egg, tadpole, water-breathing youth, liminal transition, land emergence, vocal adulthood, seasonal return.

| Metamorphic stage | Possible narrative function |
|---|---|
| Egg / spawn | origin, vulnerability, destiny, collective life |
| Tadpole | pre-identity, hidden growth, aquatic dependence |
| Limb emergence | transition, awkwardness, becoming, risk |
| First land contact | exile, maturity, danger, wonder |
| Voice / call | identity, courtship, prophecy, warning, belonging |
| Return to water | memory, ancestry, rebirth, grief, cycle completion |

Calibration rule:

> Metamorphosis can be plot, theme, character arc, symbol, and world law at the same time.

---

## Layer 14 — Mythic Eco-System Layer

Track the ecosystem as a mythic system rather than simple setting.

Required detection:

- wetlands, ponds, rivers, marshes, forests, rain, mud, eggs, insects, predators, drought, contamination, seasons, moonlight, frog song;
- sacred habitat versus exploited habitat;
- ecological imbalance as plot pressure;
- mythic memory embedded in landscape.

Calibration rule:

> In mythic eco-fantasy, habitat is not backdrop. Habitat is authority.

---

## Layer 15 — Human Intrusion / Human Kinship Layer

Track whether humans enter the amphibian world as destroyers, witnesses, exploiters, protectors, translators, descendants, invaders, or kin.

| Human function | Evaluation question |
|---|---|
| Intruder | Does human presence threaten habitat or culture? |
| Witness | Does the human perceive accurately or impose assumptions? |
| Translator | Does cross-species communication create responsibility? |
| Protector | Does protection avoid paternalism? |
| Kin / descendant | Does the human belong partly to amphibian myth or ecology? |
| Exploiter | Does the narrative expose extraction, collection, captivity, or scientific reduction? |

Calibration rule:

> Human contact must be evaluated as ecological, moral, and mythic pressure.

---

## Layer 16 — Frog Civilization / Amphibian Culture Layer

If the manuscript includes organized frog or amphibian society, evaluate it as a culture, not as a gimmick.

Required ledgers:

- governance;
- kinship;
- ritual;
- language/calls;
- migration;
- territory;
- predator relations;
- memory/history;
- taboo;
- death rites;
- metamorphosis rites.

Calibration rule:

> Nonhuman culture must have internal logic, not merely human society in frog costumes.

Failure condition: an evaluation fails if it praises imaginative frog culture without testing whether the culture has coherent survival pressures, rituals, boundaries, and social rules.

---

## Layer 17 — Cross-Species Relationship Layer

Track relationships that cross human/frog/amphibian boundaries.

| Relationship type | Evaluation requirement |
|---|---|
| Human ↔ frog witness | Does one species truly perceive the other? |
| Human ↔ amphibian guide | Is guidance reciprocal or extractive? |
| Frog ↔ human child | Does wonder preserve agency for both sides? |
| Human ↔ transformed being | Does transformation change moral obligation? |
| Frog society ↔ human society | Do the two systems collide, mirror, or heal each other? |

Calibration rule:

> Cross-species relationships must preserve otherness while creating emotional legibility.

---

## Layer 18 — Transformation Ethics Layer

Track all transformations: bodily, spiritual, social, ecological, linguistic, and perceptual.

Evaluation questions:

- Is transformation chosen, imposed, inherited, accidental, ritualized, or punished?
- Does transformation erase identity or reveal it?
- Does transformation create empathy or appropriation?
- Does transformed embodiment alter moral responsibility?

Calibration rule:

> Transformation is not automatically growth. It may be gift, wound, exile, duty, or violation.

---

## Layer 19 — Frog Symbolic Register Layer

Required symbolic systems include:

- water;
- mud;
- rain;
- eggs/spawn;
- tadpoles;
- skin/permeability;
- breath;
- voice/croak/call/song;
- leaping;
- camouflage;
- predation;
- seasonal return;
- disappearance;
- toxicity/poison;
- wetlands as memory.

Calibration rule:

> Frog symbolism must be lifecycle-traced, not merely listed.

---

## Layer 20 — Ecological Permeability Layer

Frogs are biologically porous: skin, water, contamination, temperature, habitat, and seasonal changes matter. This creates an ecological vulnerability layer.

Track:

- contamination;
- drying wetlands;
- chemical exposure;
- habitat fragmentation;
- temperature shifts;
- predators;
- silence where frog calls should be;
- malformed bodies or disrupted metamorphosis if present.

Calibration rule:

> Amphibian bodies can function as ecological witnesses.

---

## Layer 21 — Myth / Science / Wonder Tension Layer

A mythic eco-fantasy about amphibians may require both scientific and mythic interpretation.

| Knowledge mode | Evaluation requirement |
|---|---|
| Science | Observe biological, ecological, and environmental logic. |
| Myth | Track symbolic ancestry, sacred habitat, prophecy, and ritual law. |
| Wonder | Preserve reader awe without collapsing into vagueness. |
| Ecology | Treat habitat and species pressure as causality. |

Calibration rule:

> Science and myth can coexist if each has a governed function.

---

## Layer 22 — Amphibian-Human Conflict Layer

Track conflict between human systems and frog/amphibian systems.

Conflict domains:

- habitat destruction;
- scientific exploitation;
- collection/captivity;
- pollution;
- disbelief;
- language failure;
- fear/disgust;
- rescue that becomes domination;
- prophecy versus evidence;
- child wonder versus adult reduction.

Calibration rule:

> Human-frog conflict is strongest when ecological harm, moral ignorance, and mythic consequence converge.

---

## Layer 23 — Reader Translation Layer

RevisionGrade must assess how the manuscript teaches readers to understand nonhuman consciousness without over-humanizing it.

Track:

- how frog perception is rendered;
- how unfamiliar rituals become legible;
- how nonhuman stakes become emotionally meaningful;
- how the text avoids reducing frogs to pets, symbols, or jokes.

Calibration rule:

> The reader must be taught to care across species difference without erasing that difference.

## Calibration Requirements

A compliant full MythOAmphibia evaluation must identify:

- species boundary law;
- amphibian metamorphosis as plot/thematic architecture;
- frog or amphibian culture as internally governed if present;
- habitat as authority, memory, and pressure system;
- human intrusion/kinship/exploitation dynamics;
- cross-species relationship ethics;
- ecological permeability and amphibian vulnerability;
- myth/science/wonder tension;
- transformation as gift, wound, exile, duty, or violation;
- reader translation of nonhuman consciousness.

---

## Failure Conditions

A MythOAmphibia evaluation is incomplete if it:

- treats frogs as decorative fantasy creatures only;
- treats human characters as the only meaningful consciousness;
- ignores habitat as active story law;
- praises worldbuilding without testing ecological logic;
- collapses frog culture into human culture with frog names;
- treats transformation as automatically positive;
- ignores cross-species ethical boundaries;
- misses frog lifecycle, voice, skin, water, and metamorphosis as symbolic systems;
- imposes ordinary quest calibration without recognizing multi-species mythic architecture.
