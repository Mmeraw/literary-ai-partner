## Long-Form Multi-Layer Calibration Layers

This section normalizes *The Awakening* to the long-form multi-layer calibration family. It preserves `runtime-authority: false` while making the calibration lessons explicit for multi-layer evaluation behavior.

### Layer 1 — Narrative Arc Calibration

| Arc segment | Function | Calibration rule |
|---|---|---|
| Grand Isle social enclosure | Establishes vacation society, marriage role, motherhood expectation, and the first pressure between inner life and public identity. | Quiet social setting can function as the opening pressure system. |
| Swimming / bodily awakening | Edna discovers physical agency and interior possibility. | Bodily sensation can be plot escalation. |
| Desire and self-recognition | Robert, music, art, solitude, and domestic friction awaken unassimilated selfhood. | Desire should be read as catalyst for identity pressure, not romance-only structure. |
| New Orleans role refusal | Edna resists domestic performance, experiments with independence, and separates inner authority from social duty. | Interior refusal and social withdrawal can be major state changes. |
| Pigeon House / artistic and erotic autonomy | Edna tries to inhabit an alternative life beyond wife/mother role. | Partial autonomy can be structurally real even if unstable. |
| Final return to sea | Ambiguous closure resolves the conflict between imposed role and impossible freedom without conventional recovery. | Ambiguity can be artistically complete when symbolic and character ledgers converge. |

### Layer 2 — Character Arc Calibration

| Character / force | Initial state | Transformation | Final calibration obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Edna Pontellier | Socially positioned wife/mother with latent interior hunger | Awakens bodily, emotionally, artistically, erotically, and existentially | Track interior pressure as the central plot engine. |
| Léonce Pontellier | Husband, provider, social authority | Exposes domestic role expectations and property/status pressure | Treat as system pressure, not cartoon villain. |
| Robert Lebrun | Romantic/social possibility | Catalyzes desire while revealing romance cannot solve structural constraint | Do not treat him as default closure. |
| Adèle Ratignolle | Warm conventional femininity | Becomes loving contrast and social-role pressure | Distinguish affection from freedom. |

| Children / motherhood force | Care obligation and identity pressure | Intensifies Edna's selfhood conflict | Track as role pressure and felt attachment, not simple rejection. |

### Layer 3 — Theme Layer Calibration

Required thematic detection includes selfhood, marriage, motherhood, property, bodily autonomy, desire, art, solitude, social role, gendered expectation, and the cost of freedom.

Calibration rule: theme is not separate from plot; theme is the pressure system that determines what Edna can imagine, refuse, desire, or survive.

### Layer 5 — Relationship Layer Calibration

Relationship pressure is carried by role expectation, desire, care, social warmth, artistic independence, and the limits of conventional romance.

Required relationships include Edna/Léonce, Edna/Robert, Edna/Adèle, Edna/Mlle. Reisz, Edna/children/motherhood, and Edna/social world.

Calibration rule: relationships may function as pressure fields rather than mutual arcs of equal transformation.

### Layer 6 — POV Layer Calibration

*The Awakening* uses close attention to Edna's interior pressure while preserving enough narrative distance to keep the ending interpretively open.

Calibration rules:

- Interiority is not plot absence.
- Psychological pressure can escalate without external antagonist escalation.
- Narrative restraint can deepen ambiguity.
- Ambiguous closure should not be flattened into one moral or diagnostic verdict without evidence.

### Layer 7 — World Layer Calibration

Primary world systems include Grand Isle, New Orleans domestic society, Creole social codes, marriage/property expectations, motherhood norms, art/music spaces, beach/sea environment, and the Pigeon House.

Calibration rule: social world is antagonist-like pressure. The evaluator must detect role systems even when no single villain governs the conflict.

### Layer 8 — Reader Experience Layer Calibration

Expected reader progression: social observation → unease → bodily awakening → desire → self-recognition → role resistance → autonomy experiment → existential narrowing → ambiguous release or refusal.

Calibration rule: reader propulsion comes from interior escalation and symbolic convergence, not from conventional incident volume.

### Layer 9 — Market Layer Calibration

Modern shelf: literary fiction, feminist classic, psychological/social novel, interiority-driven novella/short novel, symbolic literary realism.

Calibration rule: do not impose modern commercial pacing, thriller antagonism, or conventional romantic closure. The correct calibration question is whether the novel converts social role pressure into irreversible interior transformation.

### Layer 10 — Story Ledger Layer Calibration

Required story-ledger extraction must identify: Edna's interior-pressure engine, marriage/property pressure, motherhood role conflict, Robert as catalyst-not-solution, Adèle as conventional warmth/constraint, Mlle. Reisz as independence model/warning, sea/birds/music symbolic lifecycle, Pigeon House autonomy experiment, and ambiguous closure.

Failure condition: any ledger that treats the novel as only adultery plot, only romance, only symbolic atmosphere, or only tragic ending is incomplete.

### Layer 11 — Calibration Notes Layer

RevisionGrade must recognize the following as valid craft choices in this calibration family:

- Interiority as plot.
- Social expectation as antagonistic pressure.
- Symbolic environment as structural engine.
- Bodily sensation as narrative escalation.
- Desire as self-recognition catalyst.
- Ambiguous closure as legitimate literary design.
- Quiet external action with high psychological causality.
- Relational pressure without conventional reciprocal arcs.

RevisionGrade should not recommend modernization solely because a classic uses interiority, ambiguity, symbolic recurrence, social constraint, or quiet external plotting instead of contemporary commercial incident density.
