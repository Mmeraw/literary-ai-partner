## Long-Form Multi-Layer Calibration Layers

### Layer 1 — Narrative Arc Calibration

| Arc segment | Function | Calibration rule |
|---|---|---|
| Netherfield arrival and Bennet marriage economy | Establishes money, entailment, marriage pressure, family dynamics, and comic social stakes. | Low external action can still declare the full pressure system. |
| Meryton assembly and first impressions | Public manners fix reputation and create durable misjudgment. | A dance, insult, or social slight can be a plot event. |
| Collins / Charlotte / Wickham complications | Exposes economic survival, charm-as-false-evidence, and social vulnerability. | Side relationships may carry the novel's governing argument. |
| Rosings / first proposal / Darcy letter | Converts romantic shock into evidence correction and self-recognition. | Letters can be deterministic revelation gates. |
| Pemberley re-evaluation | Place, servants, conduct, and Georgiana revise Darcy's meaning. | Estate/place can function as moral evidence. |
| Lydia scandal and Lady Catherine closure | Family negligence becomes public threat; rank coercion accidentally releases resolution. | Reputation crisis is real conflict; social repair may occur partly offstage while remaining structurally valid. |

### Layer 2 — Character Arc Calibration

| Character | Initial state | Transformation | Final calibration obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Elizabeth Bennet | Witty, perceptive, independent, quick to judge | Learns that interpretive confidence can become prejudice | Track self-correction, not independence alone. |
| Fitzwilliam Darcy | Proud, reserved, correct but ungenerous | Learns humility, action, and love without public display | Track first proposal failure, letter, Pemberley conduct, Lydia repair, and second proposal. |
| Jane Bennet | Generous, reserved, emotionally sincere | Suffers from others' misreadings and Bingley's pliability | Treat quiet goodness as structural mirror. |
| Charles Bingley | Open, amiable, influence-prone | Returns to chosen commitment | Do not flatten him into reward-object; he is amiability under social pressure. |
| Charlotte Lucas | Pragmatic friend with limited options | Chooses security over romantic ideal | Treat as economic realism and worldview pressure. |
| Lydia Bennet | Comic impulsiveness and flirtation | Becomes reputational crisis trigger | Track comedy turning into structural danger. |
| George Wickham | Charming grievance narrator | Revealed as evidence-corrupting predator | Treat charm as a plot-level false-evidence system. |
| Lady Catherine de Bourgh | Rank coercion and entitlement | Accidentally catalyzes closure | Treat as final pressure-turn, not merely comic antagonist. |

### Layer 3 — Theme Layer Calibration

Required thematic detection includes pride, prejudice, rank, money, marriage, entailment, self-knowledge, reputation, family governance, female economic vulnerability, moral generosity, and the difference between pleasing manners and trustworthy conduct.

Calibration rule: theme is embedded in social action. It appears through visits, proposals, letters, houses, dances, rumors, income, and reputational exposure rather than direct philosophical declaration.

### Layer 4 — Symbol Layer Calibration

| Symbol / system | Narrative function | Calibration rule |
|---|---|---|
| Netherfield | Marriage opportunity and social testing ground | Arrival/departure/return must be tracked as courtship-state changes. |
| Longbourn entail | Background legal threat that drives marriage pressure | Property law is plot pressure, not period decoration. |
| Meryton assembly / balls | Public reputation and first-impression engine | Social display changes story state. |
| Darcy's letter | Central evidence hinge | Written explanation can reorganize the entire moral field. |
| Pemberley | Place as moral evidence | House, grounds, servants, and conduct reveal character. |
| Brighton / regiment | Glamour masking danger | Social mobility and spectacle become reputational exposure. |
| Lady Catherine's visit | Intended coercion becomes indirect permission | A failed threat can become closure mechanism. |

### Layer 5 — Relationship Layer Calibration

Relationship pressure is carried by judgment, misjudgment, reputation, economic pressure, influence, class pride, sibling loyalty, and family exposure.

Required relationships include Elizabeth/Darcy, Jane/Bingley, Elizabeth/Jane, Elizabeth/Wickham, Elizabeth/Charlotte, Darcy/Bingley, Darcy/Wickham, Bennet parents/daughters, Lady Catherine/Elizabeth, and Gardiners/Elizabeth-Darcy.

Calibration rule: relationships may be structurally central even when they move through politeness, letters, visits, family reports, or withheld speech rather than overt confrontation.

### Layer 6 — POV Layer Calibration

*Pride and Prejudice* uses third-person narration, free indirect discourse, and Elizabeth-aligned perception while maintaining ironic distance.

Calibration rules:

- Free indirect narration is a control system.
- Wit is both character strength and interpretive vulnerability.
- Reader alignment can be deliberately partial.
- Self-correction may occur when evidence revises a character's perception, not when external action escalates.

### Layer 7 — World Layer Calibration

Primary world systems include Longbourn, Netherfield, Meryton, Rosings, Pemberley, Brighton, London, property law, entailment, marriage economics, kinship, military glamour, rank etiquette, and gossip circulation.

Calibration rule: Austen's worldbuilding is social and economic. The evaluator must not require speculative or high-action worldbuilding to recognize environmental logic.

### Layer 8 — Reader Experience Layer Calibration

Expected reader progression: amusement → social recognition → wounded judgment → romantic tension → evidence shock → self-revision → reputational anxiety → restored comic order.

Calibration rule: reader propulsion comes from changing social knowledge and moral interpretation. Dialogue, letters, visits, proposals, and refusals are state-changing actions.

### Layer 9 — Market Layer Calibration

Modern shelf: literary social comedy, courtship novel, classic romance, moral comedy, marriage-market novel, social-perception fiction.

Calibration rule: do not impose modern thriller pacing or external-conflict density. The correct calibration question is whether the novel converts perception, reputation, and marriage economics into full narrative causality.

### Layer 10 — Story Ledger Layer Calibration

Required story-ledger extraction must identify: social-perception engine, marriage-market pressure, entailment, first-impression trap, Darcy/Elizabeth reciprocal correction, Charlotte's pragmatic counterargument, Wickham's evidence corruption, Lydia as family-governance payoff, Pemberley as moral evidence, letter/reputation mechanics, and late offstage remediation cost.

Failure condition: any ledger that treats the novel as only enemies-to-lovers romance, only drawing-room comedy, or only marriage outcome is incomplete.

### Layer 11 — Calibration Notes Layer

RevisionGrade must recognize the following as valid craft choices in this calibration family:

- Dialogue as action.
- Misjudgment as plot.
- Reputation as conflict.
- Marriage economics as stakes.
- Social visits and letters as state changes.
- Free indirect narration as structural control.
- Interior correction as narrative momentum.
- Comic caricature with structural purpose.
- Low external action with high causal density.

RevisionGrade should not recommend modernization solely because a classic uses drawing-room scenes, politeness, social reports, letters, marriage plotting, comic closure, or constrained period stakes instead of contemporary external action.
