## Executive verdict

*The Wonderful Wizard of Oz* is a valuable long-form public-domain calibration text because it demonstrates that narrative simplicity is not narrative weakness. Its power comes from clear quest architecture, recurring episodic tests, symbolic companions, tonal steadiness, and a child-accessible world whose imaginative rules remain easy to grasp.

The novel's governing engine is not psychological density in the adult literary sense. It is a journey structure in which Dorothy's desire to return home gathers companions who each believe they lack a defining inner quality. The Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, and Cowardly Lion externalize questions of intelligence, feeling, and courage. The road to Oz converts those perceived lacks into repeated demonstrations that the desired qualities are already present in action.

Its calibration value is therefore anti-false-positive protection: RevisionGrade must not mistake accessibility for shallowness, archetype for flatness, episodic progression for aimlessness, or children's-register clarity for weak prose. The principal modern weakness is that some episodes are thinly motivated and some symbolic answers are more declarative than psychologically complex. But as quest fantasy and children's literature, the structure is durable, legible, and teachable.

## Long-Form Multi-Layer Calibration Layers

### Layer 1 — Narrative Arc Calibration

| Arc segment | Function | Calibration rule |
|---|---|---|
| Kansas displacement / cyclone | Establishes home, vulnerability, and portal transition. | A simple inciting incident can be structurally sufficient when it creates a clear governing desire. |
| Arrival in Oz and road assignment | Converts disorientation into a quest path. | Quest clarity is not weakness. |
| Companion gathering | Adds symbolic ensemble members whose desired qualities become repeated action tests. | Archetypal ensemble design can carry theme and plot together. |
| Road episodes and obstacles | Tests courage, intelligence, compassion, endurance, and loyalty. | Episodic structure is valid when each episode pressures the quest or companion logic. |
| Emerald City / Wizard revelation | Exposes institutional illusion and the insufficiency of external validation. | A false authority can be the structural hinge. |
| Witch conflict and final return | Converts danger into action payoff and returns Dorothy to the home promise. | Closure should be judged by promise/payoff and restored orientation. |

### Layer 2 — Character Arc Calibration

| Character / force | Initial state | Transformation | Final calibration obligation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Dorothy | Displaced child seeking home | Grows through loyalty, courage, practical action, and perseverance | Track her as quest center and moral anchor, not passive passenger. |
| Scarecrow | Believes he lacks brains | Repeatedly shows problem-solving and practical intelligence | Treat perceived lack versus demonstrated quality as the arc. |
| Tin Woodman | Believes he lacks a heart | Repeatedly shows tenderness, care, and moral feeling | Do not demand adult interiority for symbolic emotional coherence. |
| Cowardly Lion | Believes he lacks courage | Acts despite fear | Track courage as action under fear, not absence of fear. |
| Wizard | External authority and illusion | Revealed as ordinary performer/political symbol | Treat as false validation system. |
| Wicked Witch | Threat pressure | Forces action, risk, and rescue dynamics | Villain function can be structural without deep psychology. |
| Glinda / good magic figures | Guidance and closure support | Help reveal return-home logic | Treat aid figures as quest-rule functions. |

### Layer 3 — Theme Layer Calibration

Required thematic detection includes home, self-trust, friendship, courage, intelligence, compassion, false authority, illusion, practical goodness, and the idea that desired qualities may already be present before formal recognition.

Calibration rule: theme is carried by companion action and quest repetition. Do not require adult introspection for thematic coherence.

### Layer 4 — Symbol Layer Calibration

| Symbol / system | Narrative function | Calibration rule |
|---|---|---|
| Yellow Brick Road | Quest path, direction, and episodic progression | Road structure is plot architecture. |
| Silver shoes / magic shoes | Latent return-home power | Payoff may be hidden in an object present before the protagonist understands it. |
| Emerald City | Authority, spectacle, desire, and illusion | Place can reveal false power. |
| Brain / heart / courage tokens | Externalized validation of already-demonstrated qualities | Symbolic recognition can pay character arcs. |
| Kansas / home | Origin, longing, belonging, closure promise | Home is a structural promise, not merely setting. |
| Witches / directional magic | Moral geography and threat/guidance framework | Simple moral mapping can be effective in children's fantasy. |

### Layer 5 — Relationship Layer Calibration

Relationship pressure is carried by companionship, loyalty, protection, mutual need, and shared quest purpose. Required relationships include Dorothy/companions, Dorothy/Toto, companions/each other, Dorothy/Wizard, Dorothy/Wicked Witch, and Dorothy/guidance figures.

Calibration rule: friendship and ensemble loyalty can replace romance, family drama, or adult conflict as the central relationship spine.

### Layer 6 — POV Layer Calibration

The novel uses clear child-accessible narration with stable external action focus and emotionally legible stakes.

Calibration rules:

- Simplicity is not POV failure.
- Child-facing clarity is a valid voice strategy.
- Limited psychological depth can be appropriate to genre and audience.
- The evaluator must not impose adult interiority requirements on children's quest fantasy.

### Layer 7 — World Layer Calibration

Primary world systems include Kansas, Oz, Munchkin Country, Yellow Brick Road, forests/fields/obstacle spaces, Emerald City, Witch territories, magic objects, talking beings, and color-coded moral/geographic zones.

Calibration rule: worldbuilding can be episodic, iconic, and accessible rather than encyclopedic. Encountered obstacles can teach the world's rules.

### Layer 8 — Reader Experience Layer Calibration

Expected reader progression: displacement → wonder → companionship → obstacle suspense → comic/strange encounter → threat escalation → revelation → return-home satisfaction.

Calibration rule: wonder can be a narrative engine. The evaluator should measure clarity, memorability, emotional safety/danger balance, and quest payoff, not adult complexity.

### Layer 9 — Market Layer Calibration

Modern shelf: children's fantasy, portal fantasy, quest fantasy, classic children's literature, archetypal ensemble adventure.

Calibration rule: do not impose adult literary interiority, modern YA darkness, or adult fantasy lore density. The correct calibration question is whether the novel delivers coherent wonder, quest movement, symbolic companions, and homecoming payoff.

### Layer 10 — Story Ledger Layer Calibration

Required story-ledger extraction must identify: displacement/home promise, road quest, companion recruitment, perceived-lack arcs, repeated demonstration of desired qualities, Emerald City/Wizard illusion, Witch threat, hidden return-home mechanism, ensemble loyalty, and closure/payoff.

Failure condition: any ledger that treats the novel as merely episodic, merely childish, or merely whimsical without identifying quest architecture and symbolic companion payoff is incomplete.

### Layer 11 — Calibration Notes Layer

RevisionGrade must recognize the following as valid craft choices in this calibration family:

- Simplicity as strength.
- Accessibility as craft discipline.
- Archetypal characterization as valid design.
- Episodic quest progression as structure.
- Ensemble companions as symbolic and emotional architecture.
- Wonder as a narrative engine.
- Child-facing prose clarity as appropriate voice control.
- Moral/geographic simplicity as genre-appropriate world logic.

RevisionGrade should not recommend modernization solely because a classic children's fantasy uses simple prose, archetypal companions, episodic obstacles, symbolic objects, or clear moral geography instead of adult psychological realism.

## Required Detection / Failure Conditions

A DREAM evaluation of *The Wonderful Wizard of Oz* fails if it does not detect:

- Dorothy's home-return promise as the governing quest engine;
- Yellow Brick Road / episodic journey as plot architecture;
- Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, and Cowardly Lion as perceived-lack / demonstrated-quality arcs;
- Wizard as false authority / external validation system;
- silver shoes as hidden payoff object;
- Oz worldbuilding as accessible encountered logic;
- simplicity and children's-register clarity as calibrated strengths;
- episodic looseness as the main structural caveat, not total failure.
