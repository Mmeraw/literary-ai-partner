## Executive verdict

*Great Expectations* is a world-class long-form calibration text because its plot mechanics are inseparable from moral psychology. The novel's governing engine is not simply that Pip receives money and becomes a gentleman; it is that a frightened child, wounded by shame, mistakes status for transformation and then must learn the cost of despising the people who loved him before he had expectations.

The opening establishes the whole moral architecture with astonishing efficiency: Pip's name, orphanhood, tombstones, marshes, river, sea, fear, class vulnerability, convict violence, secrecy, food theft, and guilt all appear before the social plot begins. The opening gives Pip's self-naming, his derivation of family from tombstones, the marsh as first identity field, and Magwitch's terrifying demand for file and food.

The novel's greatest achievement is its long-form revelation design. Miss Havisham, Estella, Satis House, Jaggers, Wemmick, Joe, Biddy, Orlick, Compeyson, and Magwitch all form a social-moral machine around Pip's false story about himself. The major turn is not only the benefactor reveal; it is the collapse of Pip's fantasy that money, Estella, refinement, and Miss Havisham's theatrical wound all belong to one coherent destiny.

The principal weakness, from a modern long-form evaluation standpoint, is that the serial middle sometimes dilates around social rooms, minor eccentrics, debt, and urban drift before the revelation engine reasserts full pressure. That expansion is often brilliant Dickensian social worldbuilding, but a DREAM evaluator must still name the pacing cost.

Current calibration posture: canonical public-domain DREAM calibration text. It should teach RevisionGrade to detect character shame architecture, false-causality plotting, serial pacing, class mobility and moral injury, symbolic object/place lifecycle, and the difference between sentimentality that earns payoff and sentimentality that risks overstatement.

## Market / shelf description

Adult literary bildungsroman / social-moral novel in which an orphan's sudden rise into gentlemanly expectations exposes the cruelty of class shame, the false glamour of status, and the saving force of loyalty, remorse, and chosen kinship.

---

## What this manuscript should not become

- It should not be evaluated as a simple rise-and-fall social mobility plot; its real engine is shame, misrecognition, and moral education.
- It should not reduce Miss Havisham to Gothic eccentricity; she is a wound-system that manufactures Estella and miseducates Pip.
- It should not reduce Magwitch to plot twist; he is the hidden moral inversion of Pip's class fantasy.
- It should not treat Joe as comic relief; he is the novel's ethical ground.
- It should not mistake serial breadth for lack of design; breadth is part of Dickens's social method, though it carries pacing cost.
- It should not overpraise canonical status by ignoring middle dilation or softened closure ambiguity.

## Evidence Distribution / Confidence Notes

A valid DREAM evaluation of *Great Expectations* must cite evidence across:

- Opening: tombstones, marsh, Magwitch, file/food theft, Joe/Mrs. Joe household.
- Early-middle: Satis House, Estella, Miss Havisham, class humiliation, apprenticeship shame.
- Middle: Jaggers announcement, London education, Herbert, Wemmick, debt, social drift.
- Late: Magwitch reveal, Compeyson, escape/pursuit, Miss Havisham remorse/fire, Pip's illness.
- Ending: Joe/Biddy moral reckoning, Magwitch deathbed recognition, Estella/Pip ending.

High confidence cannot come only from the marsh opening or Satis House. Pip's full arc requires London, Magwitch, illness, Joe/Biddy, and Estella closure evidence.

## Required Detection / Failure Conditions

A DREAM evaluation of *Great Expectations* fails if it does not detect:

- Pip's moral/psychological arc as the central plot engine;
- social mobility and shame as structure, not theme-only commentary;
- Joe as ethical ground and load-bearing relationship spine;
- Magwitch as benefactor inversion and convict-father figure;
- Miss Havisham/Estella as miseducation architecture;
- Satis House as symbolic environment and wound-system;
- serial pacing breadth as both Dickensian strength and pacing cost;
- false-causality revelation structure around Pip's benefactor assumption;
- tactile/sensory morality: file, food, forge, hands, iron, fire, mist;
- ending as moral recovery, not simple romantic closure.

## Calibration notes for RevisionGrade

- *Great Expectations* teaches that moral psychology can be plot architecture.
- The evaluator must detect shame as an organizing system, not merely a character trait.
- A quiet character like Joe can be more structurally central than louder social figures.
- False causality can drive long-form revelation: the reader and protagonist believe the wrong story for a long time.
- Serial breadth should be evaluated with nuance: it can enrich social world and still create momentum drag.
- Symbolic places require lifecycle tracing: marsh, forge, Satis House, London, river.
- Sentimentality should be judged by whether it earns moral payoff through prior action.
- Canonical prestige must not prevent diagnosis of pacing dilation or softened closure ambiguity.
