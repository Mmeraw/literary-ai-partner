# The Murder on the Links — Multi-Layer Mystery Calibration

## Purpose

This benchmark teaches investigation architecture, clue systems, red-herring engineering, deduction quality, suspect management, reveal sequencing, and fair-play mystery construction.

## Standard Multi-Layer Structure

1. Narrative Arc Layer
2. Character Arc Layer
3. Theme Layer
4. Symbol Layer
5. Relationship Layer
6. POV Layer
7. World Layer
8. Reader Experience Layer
9. Market Layer
10. Story Ledger Layer
11. Calibration Notes Layer

## Layer 12 — Causality Layer

Required distinction:

- Apparent cause
- Actual cause

Calibration rule:

Readers solve interpretations, not events.

## Layer 13 — Information Architecture Layer

Track:

- When information becomes available.
- When information becomes understandable.
- When information becomes actionable.

Calibration rule:

Availability and understanding are not the same thing.

## Layer 14 — Reader Deduction Layer

Track reader belief progression:

- Initial suspicion
- False certainty
- Contradiction
- Theory revision
- Final understanding

Calibration rule:

Mysteries control belief more than information.

## Layer 15 — Fair-Play Layer

Questions:

- Could the attentive reader solve it?
- Was critical evidence available?
- Were rules changed late?
- Does the solution survive reread scrutiny?

Classification:

- PASS
- BORDERLINE
- FAIL

## Layer 16 — Red-Herring Engineering Layer

Good red herring:

Evidence exists.
Interpretation is wrong.

Bad red herring:

Evidence absent.
Reader artificially tricked.

Calibration rule:

The best red herrings become stronger on reread.

## Layer 17 — Sleuth Competence Layer

Evaluate:

- Observation
- Evidence handling
- Hypothesis generation
- Hypothesis revision
- Error recovery
- Deductive quality

Calibration rule:

Correct guessing is not equivalent to successful investigation.

## Layer 18 — Revelation Pressure Layer

Track:

- Curiosity
- Suspicion
- Uncertainty
- False confidence
- Contradiction
- Revelation
- Retrospective clarity

Calibration rule:

A great reveal is surprising and inevitable.

A compliant evaluation must identify:
