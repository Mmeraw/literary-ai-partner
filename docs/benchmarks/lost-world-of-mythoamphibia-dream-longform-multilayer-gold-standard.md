# The Lost World of MythOAmphibia — DREAM Long-Form Multi-Layer Gold Standard

## Governance notice

This benchmark establishes *The Lost World of MythOAmphibia / DOMINATUS I* as a native long-form multi-layer DREAM gold-standard candidate for complex mythic eco-fantasy, multi-species civilization, object-consciousness narration, ecological collapse, and distributed divine / environmental / biological consciousness.

This file is a benchmark and calibration document. It must not republish the manuscript, reproduce full chapters, reveal protected ending strategy beyond what is required for benchmark governance, or expose private rights/query/submission strategy.

The earlier architecture seed remains useful as a trace artifact, but this file is the controlling MythOAmphibia benchmark target.

---

## Executive benchmark verdict

*The Lost World of MythOAmphibia* is structurally unlike the other native benchmarks. It is not only fantasy, eco-horror, satire, or animal civilization fiction. It is a multi-scale mythic ecology in which humans, amphibians, objects, relics, mines, gods, habitats, glyphs, and memory systems all participate in the story’s causality.

The benchmark therefore exists to teach RevisionGrade that a manuscript can distribute consciousness across biological characters, nonhuman societies, objects, environmental systems, sacred artifacts, and divine voices without losing structural seriousness. The evaluator must not flatten the manuscript into “frog fantasy,” “gross-out horror,” “environmental allegory,” or “animal adventure.” Its challenge is that all of those modes are present, but none alone is sufficient.

Core calibration sentence:

> MythOAmphibia is a multi-species civilizational eco-mythology where ecological harm, bodily transformation, object memory, amphibian survival, human violence, divine ambiguity, and realm-level consciousness form one governed narrative system.

## DREAM canonical core layers

### Layer 1 — Narrative Arc Calibration

Required detection: the story begins in desecration, infection, and ecological violation, then expands into amphibian civilizational crisis, human violence, cross-species conflict, survival strategy, doctrinal fracture, and migration / continuation.

Calibration rule: the arc is not a single protagonist quest. It is a contamination-to-civilization-to-survival arc distributed across Brutus, Billy, Crown Hyla, Thorander, Zimeon, Rana, amphibian society, the Realm, the Mine, and memory-bearing objects.

### Layer 2 — Character Arc Calibration

Required major character systems include Brutus, Billy, Crown Hyla, Thorander, Zimeon, Rana, Mage, Arcana, Enoch, Molly, Phyto, Lumen, Maalik / internal entity pressures where applicable, and major human family/incursion figures.

Calibration rule: some characters are individuals, some are institutional actors, some are bridge figures, and some are consciousness vessels. Do not apply the same arc expectations to every node.

### Layer 3 — Theme Layer Calibration

Required themes: dominion versus domination, survival versus purity, extinction, ecological consequence, shame, bodily violation, stewardship, contamination, faith, doctrinal power, transformation, kinship, legacy, and whether civilization survives by preserving itself or changing itself.

Calibration rule: theme is enacted through bodies, water, eggs, glyphs, objects, poisons, rituals, and predation. It is not merely stated by divine or narrator commentary.

### Layer 4 — Symbol Layer Calibration

Required symbolic systems: mine, Drop, toadstone, glyphs, boot, McDiaper/groin cloth, gun, knife, tent, sleeping bags, tadpoles, eggs, water, mud, frogs, goldfish, bats, spores, fungal bloom, rainbow slicks, Crown, Gorf, Anti-Gorf, and the Realm.

Calibration rule: symbols are lifecycle systems. A symbol must be tracked from first contact through transformation, transfer, corruption, sanctification, or payoff.

### Layer 5 — Relationship Layer Calibration

Required relationships: Brutus/Billy, Brutus/Mine, Brutus/Maalik or internal entity pressure, Billy/object witnesses, Crown Hyla/Thorander, Crown Hyla/council, Crown Hyla/Zimeon, Thorander/Rana, Zimeon/Maximilian, frog civilization/humans, frogs/gods, objects/owners, Realm/humans.

Calibration rule: many relationship engines are asymmetrical, coercive, ritualized, ecological, or nonhuman. Do not require conventional romance/family/friendship structures.

### Layer 6 — POV Layer Calibration

Required POV domains include human POV, amphibian POV, divine POV, realm/environmental POV, object POV, relic POV, collective/choral POV, and narrator/glyphic commentary.

Calibration rule: the truth of the manuscript is polyphonic. No single POV owns the story’s truth.

### Layer 7 — World Layer Calibration

World systems include Bralorne, MOTHERLODE Mine, Kingdom Lake, Aqua World, sacred ponds, nurseries, hibernation basins, quarries, migration routes, glyphic systems, Gorf theology, amphibian social law, human recreational space, toxic industrial residues, and sacred/relic geographies.

Calibration rule: worldbuilding is ecological, religious, bodily, and political at once.

### Layer 8 — Reader Experience Layer Calibration

Expected reader progression: disgust → dread → recognition → wonder → civilizational immersion → ethical discomfort → mythic awe → ecological grief → horror → survival suspense → strange reverence.

Calibration rule: tonal hybridity is intentional. The evaluator must distinguish productive tonal fusion from uncontrolled tonal noise.

### Layer 9 — Market Layer Calibration

Modern shelf: mythic eco-fantasy, dark ecological fantasy, literary eco-horror, adult speculative fantasy, animal-civilization myth, grotesque body-horror, environmental allegory, and transmedia/codex-ready mythos fiction.

Calibration rule: do not force middle-grade animal fantasy, standard horror, standard eco-thriller, or conventional epic fantasy expectations onto this manuscript.

### Layer 10 — Story Ledger Layer Calibration

The story ledger must capture: desecration/infection, human cruelty, object memory, amphibian extinction crisis, toadstone authority, interbreeding/adaptation, Zimeon bridge function, frog governance, glyphic doctrine, realm memory, human-amphibian conflict, and survival migration.

### Layer 11 — Calibration Notes Layer

RevisionGrade must recognize as valid craft choices: nonhuman POV, object POV, grotesque comedy, sacred codex structure, verse-code organization, glyphic recurrence, mythic biology, distributed consciousness, environmental agency, and multi-scale narrative movement.

## MythOAmphibia specialty layers

### Layer 12 — Verse Code / Sacred Fragment Architecture

The manuscript’s verse-code system replaces ordinary scene breaks and functions as scripture lookup, pacing, cross-reference, game trigger, and doctrine anchor.

Calibration rule: verse codes are not decorative formatting. They are story infrastructure.

### Layer 13 — Glyphic Governance Architecture

Glyphs must be tracked as recurring signals of possession, memory, doctrine, authority, realm response, and codex activation.

Calibration rule: a glyph is a governed sign, not a mood mark.

### Layer 14 — Species Boundary Architecture

Track human, frog, toad, amphibian, fish, bat, predator, object, deity, mine, realm, and hybrid boundaries.

Calibration rule: species difference is narrative law.

### Layer 15 — Amphibian Metamorphosis Architecture

Track egg, tadpole, polliwog, limb emergence, hibernation, breeding, maturity, disease, mutation, and hybridization.

Calibration rule: metamorphosis is plot, theme, symbol, biology, and doctrine simultaneously.

### Layer 16 — Frog Civilization Architecture

Evaluate Aqua World as a civilization with governance, rank, elders, rituals, health systems, succession, doctrine, social roles, education, taboos, and military/defensive logic.

Calibration rule: frog civilization must be evaluated as culture, not novelty.

### Layer 17 — Human Civilization / Giant Architecture

Humans are seen not only as people but as giants, pollutants, hunters, recreational intruders, false gods, careless dominators, and ecological forces.

Calibration rule: human ordinariness becomes mythic violence when seen from amphibian scale.

### Layer 18 — Mine Consciousness Architecture

The MOTHERLODE Mine must be evaluated as setting, entity, womb, tomb, lover, archive, infection vector, and appetite.

Calibration rule: the mine is not backdrop. The mine acts.

### Layer 19 — Triune / Distributed Consciousness Architecture

Darkness, Dampness, and Silence function as coordinated entity forces.

Calibration rule: distributed consciousness can have agency without being a single character.

### Layer 20 — Object Consciousness Architecture

Required object POV / object-memory systems include Boot, McDiaper/groin cloth, sleeping bags, tent, gun, knife, flashlight, horseshoe/pin, and relics where present.

Calibration rule: objects are memory containers, witness devices, shame archives, and ritual vessels.

### Layer 21 — McDiaper / Groin Cloth Architecture

McDiaper must be treated as an object-consciousness relic formed through shame, bodily fluids, memory, death, handling, and later sacralization.

Calibration rule: abjection can become relic authority when the narrative governs the transformation.

### Layer 22 — Relic and Sacred Object Architecture

Track toadstones, crown markers, glyph-bearing objects, carried objects, weapons, tools, cloths, and inherited artifacts.

Calibration rule: relics connect authority, memory, body, and civilization.

### Layer 23 — Ecological Consequence Architecture

Track meth waste, fuel, oil slicks, chemical runoff, poisoned water, frog blight, eggs failing, malformed or altered bodies, fish/goldfish disruption, and habitat collapse.

Calibration rule: contamination is causality.

### Layer 24 — Extinction Pressure Architecture

Track fertility decline, failed eggs, disease, predator imbalance, population collapse, migration urgency, and the anxiety of being the last generation.

Calibration rule: extinction is not a theme alone. It is a structural timer.

### Layer 25 — Species Survival / Hybridization Architecture

Track interbreeding, purity anxiety, hybrid vigor, cultural dilution, future generations, Rana, Zimeon, Thorander, Crown Hyla, and political consequences.

Calibration rule: survival may require biological and cultural transformation.

### Layer 26 — Zimeon Bridge Character Architecture

Zimeon is a bridge between Columbian/Orgon, old/new, purity/hybridization, tradition/adaptation, child/future ruler, and body/doctrine.

Calibration rule: bridge characters are evolutionary junction points.

### Layer 27 — Rana Successor / Counterpart Architecture

Rana must be tracked as counterpart, sibling, future-line pressure, alternative inheritance, and potential parallel/contrast to Zimeon.

Calibration rule: successor characters may carry more future weight than current page-time suggests.

### Layer 28 — Crown Hyla Governance Architecture

Track Hyla as ruler, mother, priestly authority, political strategist, survival pragmatist, liar when necessary, and body under ecological pressure.

Calibration rule: sovereignty is moral pressure, not merely status.

### Layer 29 — Thorander Ideology Architecture

Track Thorander as outsider, Orgon survivor, warrior-healer, patriarchal pressure, ideological disruptor, father, and survival radical.

Calibration rule: dangerous knowledge may still be useful knowledge.

### Layer 30 — Council / Institutional Debate Architecture

Track Mage, Arcana, Enoch, Molly, Phyto, Lumen, and other council figures as institutional voices rather than background characters.

Calibration rule: councils are argument engines.

### Layer 31 — Billy POV / Human Witness Architecture

Billy must be tracked as witness, friend, threatened body, shame/secret carrier, moral contrast, and human-scale reader surrogate.

Calibration rule: witness POV can expose change in another character before that character understands it.

### Layer 32 — Brutus Possession / Degradation Architecture

Brutus must be tracked across addiction, desecration, infection, cruelty, fear, bodily transformation, violence, and ritualized self-mythologizing.

Calibration rule: degradation is not random escalation when bodily, ecological, and mythic systems converge.

### Layer 33 — Divine Perspective Architecture

Track God, Gorf, Anti-Gorf, Sun Gorf, Water Gorf, Terra Firma Gorf, Wind Gorf, and divine ambiguity.

Calibration rule: deity language may encode ecology, doctrine, power, and misinterpretation at once.

### Layer 34 — Religious Authority / Toadstone Legitimacy Architecture

Track who speaks for Gorf, who controls the toadstone, who interprets signs, who benefits from doctrine, and who is silenced by sacred authority.

Calibration rule: religious authority is political architecture.

### Layer 35 — Realm Consciousness Architecture

Track Earth, Realm, lake, forest, mine, water, and environmental memory as observing and responding systems.

Calibration rule: the Realm is not passive setting. It remembers.

### Layer 36 — Human-Amphibian Contact Architecture

Track contact events: capture, killing, feeding to dogs, egg desecration, netting, flares, hunting, campground contamination, and moments of mutual observation.

Calibration rule: contact is rarely neutral; it changes ecological, moral, and mythic state.

### Layer 37 — Cross-Species Ethics Architecture

Track dominion versus domination, care versus exploitation, rescue versus control, protection versus paternalism, and whether humans can recognize amphibian personhood.

Calibration rule: the central ethical question is not whether humans have power, but how they use it.

### Layer 38 — Transformation Ethics Architecture

Track infection, mutation, possession, metamorphosis, hybridization, bodily violation, self-directed change, forced change, and sacred change.

Calibration rule: transformation may be gift, wound, exile, adaptation, punishment, or violation.

### Layer 39 — Myth / Science / Wonder Tension Architecture

Track biological fact, ecological logic, sacred interpretation, glyphic mystery, and reader wonder without flattening any into the other.

Calibration rule: science and myth can coexist if each has governed function.

### Layer 40 — Polyphonic Truth Architecture

Track how Brutus, Billy, Zimeon, Hyla, Thorander, Rana, council, objects, deities, mine, Realm, and narrator each hold partial truth.

Calibration rule: no single narrator owns truth.

### Layer 41 — Dominion vs Domination Architecture

Track the difference between stewardship, ownership, exploitation, indifference, cruelty, survival, and sacred responsibility.

Calibration rule: dominion becomes domination when power severs itself from care.

### Layer 42 — Narrative Scale Architecture

Track movement among individual, pair, family, camp, colony, species, ecosystem, realm, deity, and mythos scale.

Calibration rule: scale transitions must remain legible.

### Layer 43 — Ecological Memory Architecture

Track what places remember: mine, lake, swamp, sacred pond, campground, tent, water, mud, and objects.

Calibration rule: memory can be stored outside human minds.

### Layer 44 — Horror / Comedy / Sacred Register Fusion

Track grotesque humor, profanity, bodily shame, divine commentary, ritual language, and ecological dread.

Calibration rule: tonal collision can be craft when it produces layered reader response.

### Layer 45 — Transmedia / Codex Architecture

Track verse codes, glyphs, scripture cards, hidden doctrine, game mechanics, and ARG/board-game hooks.

Calibration rule: transmedia hooks must reinforce narrative meaning, not replace it.

## Calibration requirements

A compliant MythOAmphibia DREAM evaluation must identify:

1. Verse code and glyph systems as governed narrative infrastructure.
2. Mine agency and Triune distributed consciousness.
3. Object POV as legitimate narrative architecture.
4. McDiaper/groin cloth as relic-memory system, not comic throwaway.
5. Billy, Brutus, Crown Hyla, Thorander, Zimeon, and Rana as separate structural pillars.
6. Frog civilization as governed culture with institutions and political conflict.
7. Hybridization and extinction as central survival architecture.
8. Ecological contamination as direct causality.
9. Human/amphibian contact as ethical, biological, and mythic pressure.
10. Realm/Earth environmental memory as active story law.
11. Divine/Gorf systems as both faith and political interpretation.
12. Tonal fusion of grotesque comedy, horror, sacred language, and ecological grief.
13. Polyphonic truth across humans, frogs, objects, gods, and environments.

---

## Failure conditions

A MythOAmphibia evaluation is incomplete if it:

- treats the manuscript as simple frog fantasy;
- ignores Billy or Zimeon as major POV/structural pillars;
- treats Brutus only as villain or addict and misses possession/degradation architecture;
- treats frogs as cute, comic, or symbolic only;
- ignores frog governance, council debate, and species survival politics;
- fails to track object POV and relic memory;
- treats McDiaper/groin cloth as a joke rather than abjection-to-relic transformation;
- ignores ecological contamination as plot causality;
- treats the Mine, Realm, or Triune as atmosphere only;
- collapses divine/Gorf language into simple religion without testing doctrine, authority, and ecology;
- misses verse-code/glyph systems as transmedia/codex infrastructure;
- imposes ordinary single-protagonist or ordinary quest calibration.
