## Executive Verdict

*Let the River Decide* is a serious, atmospheric, adult literary eco-thriller / eco-spiritual road novel built around a memorable central proposition: water is not inert background, but witness, archive, nervous system, and judge. The book begins with road-trip unease, missing-person posters, a black truck marked NV115, a remote river camp, and the vulnerability of two travelers with dogs at the edge of the Liard. It then widens into a layered investigation of waterways, hydro development, corporate extraction, Indigenous sovereignty, memory, kinship, grief, and the possibility that the natural world has begun selecting its own form of redress.

The manuscript's greatest assets are atmosphere, voice, thematic seriousness, environmental imagination, the Mike/Cliff relationship, the animal-sensory layer, and the repeated transformation of ordinary travel detail into moral pressure. Smokehouse Camp is the book's strongest arena: fish, smoke, dogs, children, elders, weather, stones, bannock, bark maps, the longhouse, Leanna's return, and the river's silence create the kind of place-specific texture that can hold a novel.

The principal drag is not lack of plot or lack of ambition. It is over-evidence. The manuscript repeatedly tries to prove what the scenes already make the reader feel. Research files, corporate case studies, regulatory language, disappearance ledgers, and narrator conclusions sometimes press too hard against the ambiguity that makes the premise uncanny. The book is most powerful when the river remains exact but not fully legible; it is weaker when the narrator supplies the governing theory too directly.

**Benchmark verdict:** close but not yet submission-ready. The manuscript needs targeted revision around evidence compression, ambiguity control, cultural permission boundaries, Leanna/Verdant causality, NV115 payoff, and Plunkett/Anthony integration.

### Stack Diagnosis

The strongest version of the book is not “Mike solves a mystery.” It is “Mike learns the difference between investigating, witnessing, and taking.” The novel's governing tension is not whether the river is literally alive; it is whether a damaged world can still issue judgment through systems humans insist on treating as resources.

The most fragile layer is the investigation ledger. It gives the book scale and seriousness, but too often it explains the book's pattern instead of letting scene pressure reveal it. This is where revision can yield the largest quality gain without harming the manuscript's identity.

## Prioritized Revision Plan

1. **Compress the evidence ledger by 15–25%.** Keep the strongest case studies and convert the rest into a tighter dossier pattern.
2. **Promote Leanna / Verdant earlier.** Leanna is too structurally important to arrive as late as she does.
3. **Clarify NV115's role.** Decide whether NV115 is criminal threat, corporate surveillance, river-marked omen, or misread paranoia.
4. **Run a witness/ownership revision pass.** Ask whether the scene can dramatize refusal, correction, silence, or consequence instead of explanation.
5. **Commission cultural/protocol review before publication.** This is not optional for readiness.

**Revision order:** structural compression → Leanna/Verdant causality → witness/ownership boundary → cultural review → motif/prose compression → market positioning.
