## Part 9 -- Summary verdict

**Verdict: REJECTED -- civilization flattening with consciousness-domain collapse.**

The offending evaluation reduces MythOAmphibia's 45-layer, 6-domain consciousness architecture to "imaginative frog fantasy with experimental POV." This is the evaluator equivalent of treating a cathedral as a large room.

Root cause: the evaluator's conceptual model has only two consciousness categories (human and animal). Objects are props, environments are settings, divine systems are "religion," and collective entities are atmosphere. This two-category model cannot perceive the manuscript's architecture.

The syndrome manifests as:
1. **Consciousness-domain collapse:** 6 domains reduced to 2 (human + "frog").
2. **Civilization flattening:** Aqua World governance, law, doctrine, succession treated as "imaginative worldbuilding" without testing internal logic.
3. **Object blindness:** Object consciousness dismissed as whimsy or experiment.
4. **Environmental passivization:** Mine, Realm, ecological memory treated as setting.
5. **Infrastructure invisibility:** Verse codes, glyphs, transmedia hooks treated as formatting.
6. **Entity collapse:** Distinct characters (Zimeon, Rana) merged; council figures invisible.
7. **Causality substitution:** Enacted theme (contamination drives plot) misread as stated theme ("environmental concerns").

**Correction requires:** The evaluator must expand its consciousness-domain model from 2 categories to 6 and evaluate each domain's internal logic, cross-domain relationships, and structural function.
