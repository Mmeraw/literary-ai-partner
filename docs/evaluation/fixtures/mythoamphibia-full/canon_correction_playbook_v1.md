## Scoring governance rules

### Rule 1 -- Multi-species consciousness gate

An evaluation with fewer than 4 of 6 consciousness domains identified is STRUCTURALLY_INCOMPLETE and must be rejected. The evaluator must detect and separately track: human, amphibian, object, environmental, divine/doctrinal, and collective consciousness.

### Rule 2 -- Civilization seriousness requirement

Frog civilization must be evaluated as culture with internal logic -- governance, law, doctrine, succession, military/defensive logic, education, taboos, and social roles. Describing Aqua World as "imaginative" or "creative" without testing its institutional logic is CIVILIZATION_FLATTENING.

### Rule 3 -- Object consciousness integrity

Object POV must be evaluated as a governed narrative system. Objects are memory containers, witness devices, shame archives, and ritual vessels. Treating object consciousness as whimsy, experiment, or decorative choice is CANON_FALSE.

### Rule 4 -- Mine agency requirement

The MOTHERLODE Mine must be evaluated as an agentive entity -- setting AND entity AND womb AND tomb AND lover AND archive AND infection vector AND appetite. Treating the Mine as "a mine" or "an abandoned mine" without agency is ENVIRONMENTAL_PASSIVIZATION.

### Rule 5 -- Causality over theme

Ecological contamination is causality (drives plot events), not theme (provides meaning). The evaluator must track contamination through chemical dumping, egg failure, blight, habitat collapse, and population decline as plot-driving events, not thematic observations.

### Rule 6 -- McDiaper relic integrity gate

McDiaper / groin cloth must be treated as an object-consciousness relic formed through shame, bodily fluids, memory, death, handling, and later sacralization. Treating McDiaper as a joke, comic prop, or throwaway detail is CANON_FALSE. Abjection can become relic authority when the narrative governs the transformation.

### Rule 7 -- Entity individuation requirement

Zimeon and Rana are distinct characters with distinct structural functions (bridge character and successor counterpart respectively). Merging them into "young frog characters" or equivalent is ENTITY_COLLAPSE. Council figures (Mage, Arcana, Enoch, Molly, Phyto, Lumen) are institutional voices with debate functions, not background characters.

### Rule 8 -- Verse code infrastructure rule

Verse codes are story infrastructure. They must be evaluated as scripture lookup, pacing devices, cross-references, game triggers, and doctrine anchors. Treating verse codes as decorative formatting or scene breaks is INFRASTRUCTURE_INVISIBILITY. Recommending "cut the verse codes" is CANON_FALSE.

## Correction procedures

### Procedure 1 -- Consciousness domain expansion

**Trigger:** Evaluator detects only human and "frog/animal" consciousness.

**Correction steps:**

1. Re-read the manuscript tracking every passage where a non-human, non-animal entity acts, perceives, remembers, or influences events.
2. For each passage, classify: is this human, amphibian, object, environmental, divine/doctrinal, or collective consciousness?
3. Build a domain register: list each entity and its consciousness domain.
4. Verify that at least 4 of 6 domains are represented with specific textual evidence.
5. Re-score POV & Voice Control to reflect the polyphonic architecture.
6. Re-score Worldbuilding to reflect the multi-domain consciousness system.

**Verification:** Domain register contains entries in at least 4 categories with manuscript citations.

### Procedure 2 -- Civilization seriousness upgrade

**Trigger:** Frog civilization described as "imaginative" or "creative" without institutional analysis.

**Correction steps:**

1. Inventory all frog/amphibian institutions: Crown, council, toadstone, nursery/eggs, hibernation systems, healing systems.
2. For each institution, identify: governance function, risk/failure mode, doctrinal connection, succession implications.
3. Track council debate as argument engine: which positions are represented, what pressures drive each faction, what is at stake.
4. Re-evaluate Crown Hyla as sovereign under moral pressure, not generic ruler.
5. Re-score Worldbuilding to reflect governed civilization, not decorative world.

**Verification:** At least 4 institutions identified with governance function and risk.

### Procedure 3 -- Object consciousness recovery

**Trigger:** Object POV dismissed as whimsy, experiment, or decoration.

**Correction steps:**

1. Inventory all objects with POV or memory function: Boot, McDiaper, sleeping bag, tent, gun, knife, flashlight, horseshoe/pin, relics.
2. For each object, track: first function, transformation event, later/final function.
3. Identify what each object can know, remember, want, or influence.
4. Distinguish object consciousness from human consciousness: objects register through sensory domains (pressure, smell, touch, temperature, owner history), not abstract intention.
5. Re-evaluate McDiaper specifically: track abjection-to-relic transformation through shame, bodily fluids, memory, death, carrying, and sacralization.
6. Re-score POV & Voice Control and Worldbuilding to reflect object-consciousness domain.

**Verification:** Object consciousness ledger contains at least 3 objects with tracked transformation and governed rules.

### Procedure 4 -- Mine agency recovery

**Trigger:** MOTHERLODE Mine treated as setting/location only.

**Correction steps:**

1. Re-read all Mine passages tracking: when does the Mine perceive? When does it act? When does it remember? When does it desire?
2. Identify the Mine's multiple functions: setting, entity, womb, tomb, lover, archive, infection vector, appetite.
3. Track the Triune (Darkness, Dampness, Silence) as coordinated entity forces hosted by the Mine.
4. Distinguish Mine consciousness from Triune consciousness: the Mine is the vessel; the Triune is the distributed entity system within it.
5. Re-evaluate the Brutus-Mine relationship as possession/infection, not "man enters mine."
6. Re-score Worldbuilding and Character Depth to reflect Mine agency.

**Verification:** Mine tracked across at least 3 distinct agentive functions with manuscript citations.

### Procedure 5 -- Ecological causality recovery

**Trigger:** Ecological contamination treated as theme rather than plot causality.

**Correction steps:**

1. Inventory all contamination events: chemical dumping, rainbow slicks/oil, egg failure, blight/fungus, goldfish/invasive fish, silence/missing animals.
2. For each event, track: what plot consequences follow? What character decisions change? What institutional responses occur?
3. Distinguish contamination-as-causality (drives events) from contamination-as-theme (provides meaning). Both exist; the causality function must be primary.
4. Track the extinction timer: egg failure rate, population decline, habitat contraction, migration urgency.
5. Re-score Pacing to reflect extinction timer as macro-pacing device.
6. Re-score Thematic Integration to reflect enacted theme rather than stated theme.

**Verification:** At least 3 contamination events tracked with specific plot consequences.

### Procedure 6 -- Entity individuation recovery

**Trigger:** Zimeon and Rana merged into "young frog characters"; council figures omitted.

**Correction steps:**

1. Separate Zimeon and Rana into distinct character entries with distinct structural functions.
2. For Zimeon: identify bridge-character function (Columbian/Orgon, old/new, purity/hybridization, child/future ruler). Track as evolutionary junction point.
3. For Rana: identify successor-counterpart function (future-line pressure, alternative inheritance, parallel to Zimeon). Track potential future weight.
4. Inventory council figures: Mage, Arcana, Enoch, Molly, Phyto, Lumen. For each, identify institutional voice function and debate position.
5. Re-score Character Depth to reflect the full ensemble across consciousness domains.

**Verification:** Zimeon and Rana tracked as separate entries with distinct functions; at least 3 council figures identified.

### Procedure 7 -- Infrastructure recognition recovery

**Trigger:** Verse codes treated as formatting; glyphs treated as decoration.

**Correction steps:**

1. Inventory all verse-code occurrences. For each, identify function: scripture lookup, pacing, cross-reference, game trigger, or doctrine anchor.
2. Inventory all glyph occurrences. For each, classify: possession marker, memory signal, doctrine signal, authority marker, realm response, or codex activation.
3. Track how verse codes create structural rhythm distinct from chapter/scene structure.
4. Track how glyphs recur and accumulate meaning across the manuscript.
5. Re-score Scene Construction and Pacing to reflect verse-code structural rhythm.
6. Re-score Worldbuilding to reflect glyphic governance as narrative infrastructure.

**Verification:** At least 3 verse-code functions and 3 glyph functions identified with manuscript evidence.
