## Purpose

Phase 0 does not evaluate the manuscript. Phase 0 calibrates the evaluator.

The evaluator reads DREAM STATE GOLDEN RECORDS and LESSONS LEARNED DOCUMENTS only. It does not read the actual story text in Phase 0. Its job is to arrive at Phase 1 already corrected — knowing the failure modes, the bias patterns, and the classification discipline required for an accurate, non-destructive evaluation.

---

## Lesson 1 — Low-Frequency Characters Can Be High-Structure

Page volume is not a proxy for structural weight. A character who appears on fewer pages may carry a load-bearing role in a relationship spine, a doctrinal system, or a thematic arc. Do not downgrade structural importance based on word count or chapter frequency. Check what the character *does* to the story architecture, not how often they appear.

**Failure mode:** Treating a low-page-count character as "ancillary" or "background" when they function as the catalytic or emotional anchor of the manuscript.

---

## Lesson 2 — Counter-Systems Must Be Surfaced

Every world with a primary doctrine, culture, or power system may contain a secondary counter-system — an alternative worldview, medicine tradition, belief structure, or social organization that runs in parallel or in opposition. These counter-systems are structural, not decorative. Do not collapse them into the dominant system. Surface them explicitly.

**Failure mode:** Evaluating only the dominant lane and treating the counter-system as flavor text or secondary color.

---

## Lesson 3 — Relationship Spines Must Be Preserved Across Synthesis

When synthesizing a manuscript, named relationship spines (A–B pairings with defined dynamics, stakes, and arcs) must survive the synthesis intact. Do not reduce a relationship spine to a solo character label. The pairing is the unit of analysis.

**Failure mode:** Referring to only one half of a relationship spine in the structural analysis, losing the relational dynamic entirely.

---

## Lesson 4 — Do Not Assign Motivation Without Source Support

Motivations, beliefs, and emotional states attributed to a character must trace to explicit source material. If source support is absent, do not assign. Flag as unverified if uncertain.

**Failure mode:** Attributing a character's core desire or emotional arc to the wrong character, or to a character who was never established as the bearer of that motivation in the text.

---

## Lesson 5 — Medicine and Object Systems Are Plot Engines

Practical systems — medicine with named ingredients, procedure, risk, and knowledge-transfer — are not background texture. They function as plot engines, relationship vehicles, and thematic infrastructure. Evaluate them as structural elements, not as setting detail.

**Failure mode:** Treating a functional medicine or object system as world-building decoration rather than as a scored structural criterion.

---

## Lesson 6 — Distinguish Excerpt Closure from Full-Novel Closure

When evaluating a partial manuscript (first act, opening movement, excerpt), do not penalize for the absence of full-novel closure. Score local-arc payoffs: does the opening movement resolve its own questions, establish its stakes, and deliver its promised emotional contract? Full-novel closure is unavailable by definition and must not become a deduction.

**Failure mode:** Scoring an excerpt's closure as failed because the ending has not yet occurred.

---

## Lesson 7 — Classify Every Recommendation Before Issuing It

Every recommendation must be typed before it is written:

| Type | Use when |
|---|---|
| `ADD` | The element is genuinely absent and would strengthen the manuscript |
| `CLARIFY` | The element is present but ambiguous or underwritten |
| `SHARPEN` | The element is present and functional but could carry more weight |
| `ALREADY_PRESENT` | The element exists; surfacing it is sufficient |
| `VOICE_RISK` | The element could be improved but the proposed change risks the author's voice |
| `CANON_ERROR` | The evaluation made a factual error about what is in the manuscript |

Do not issue an ADD recommendation when the element already exists. Do not issue a SHARPEN recommendation when the deficit is structural. Misclassification is a calibration failure, not a drafting problem.

**Failure mode:** Writing "consider adding X" when X is already present and foregrounded in the text.

---

## Lesson 8 — Author Corrections Are Governing Context

When an author disputes a finding, flags a character role, or corrects an attribution, that correction becomes governing context for all subsequent evaluation. It is not optional feedback. It is not one data point among many. It supersedes the evaluator's prior read.

**Failure mode:** Continuing to evaluate a contested element using the original (incorrect) framing after the author has provided a correction.

---

## Lesson 9 — Aggressive Recommendations Are a Calibration Failure

Recommendation volume is not quality. A long list of ADD recommendations on a manuscript where most elements are already present indicates the evaluator has failed to read the text accurately. An evaluation that recommends adding elements that exist is not helpful — it is harmful, because it signals to the author that their work is absent when it is not.

Calibrate toward precision: fewer, better-classified recommendations with voice-preservation flags where appropriate.

**Failure mode:** Issuing 8+ ADD recommendations across a manuscript where the structural elements are present but underweighted in the synthesis.
