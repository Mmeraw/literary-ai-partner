# The Black Box Problem / RevisionGrade™ Readiness Standard

## Canonical purpose

RevisionGrade exists to help writers separate manuscript readiness from market rejection.

Publishing often gives writers verdicts without diagnoses: silence, template rejection, or no useful explanation. A writer may not know whether the issue is manuscript readiness, agent fit, market timing, list fit, or taste.

The Black Box Problem is the uncertainty created when all of those outcomes look identical from the outside.

RevisionGrade addresses the manuscript-readiness side of that uncertainty. It does not control agent taste, market timing, industry demand, or representation outcomes.

## The Black Box

I wrote four novels. Like many writers, I sent them into the publishing industry's black box. At best, I received a template rejection. More often, I received silence.

Agents are busy; they are not required to provide feedback. A manuscript may be rejected because the writing is not ready, because the story is not right for an agent's list, because the timing is off, or because the market is not looking for that kind of book.

From the outside, all of those answers look the same: no diagnosis, no explanation, no way to know whether the next step is revision or a different door.

## Verdicts without diagnosis

Publishing gives writers verdicts, not diagnoses.

Without a diagnosis, a writer cannot systematically improve the work. The result is a costly loop: revise blindly, query again, wait again, and still not know whether the manuscript is structurally failing or simply mismatched to that agent, list, or moment.

## The separation principle

RevisionGrade helps separate two variables that define a writer's outcome:

1. **Manuscript Readiness** — Is the work structurally sound, psychologically coherent, and narratively disciplined?
2. **Market Fit** — Is this the right agent, the right market, and the right timing?

RevisionGrade does not control the market. It helps isolate the manuscript-readiness question before the author sends the work into a system that may not explain why it failed.

## The RevisionGrade™ Readiness Standard

Serious manuscripts deserve structural validation before submission.

RevisionGrade evaluates manuscript readiness across thirteen core dimensions of long-form narrative:

- Concept
- Narrative Drive
- Character
- Voice
- Scene Construction
- Dialogue
- Theme
- Worldbuilding
- Pacing
- Prose Control
- Tone
- Narrative Closure
- Marketability

The Readiness Standard does not exist to generate more words. It exists to diagnose structural friction first. Any revision assistance must remain evidence-bound, author-controlled, and voice-preserving.

## What RevisionGrade does not promise

Governance requires boundaries.

RevisionGrade does not guarantee:

- representation
- publication
- agent fit
- market timing
- commercial success
- industry demand

RevisionGrade does not replace the writer's creative judgment. It does not make promises about agent taste or industry timing. It does not position itself as a generic AI content generator.

## Roadmap filter

Future features should be evaluated against this question:

> Does the feature help an author understand or improve manuscript readiness, or does it merely generate more text?
