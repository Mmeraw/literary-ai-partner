# Revise Candidate Contract

**Authority:** Revision Governance Volume VII  
**Scope:** Candidate A/B/C generation and presentation.  
**Principle:** Candidate labels are implementation details. All candidates must first satisfy Revision Authority.

## Candidate A — Conservative Repair
Purpose: minimal intervention, lowest risk, highest preservation.

Rules:
- Preserve author voice and sentence architecture where possible.
- Repair only the targeted weakness.
- Introduce no new facts, locations, timeline changes, or character knowledge.
- Prefer restraint over embellishment.

## Candidate B — Recommended Repair
Purpose: best overall solution and default TrustedPath option.

Rules:
- Balance improvement with preservation.
- Most directly satisfy the revision objective.
- Maintain continuity and surrounding transitions.
- Be the safest default for one-click application.

## Candidate C — Aggressive Repair
Purpose: higher-impact alternative for authors who want more change.

Rules:
- May restructure more strongly than A or B.
- Must still preserve voice, canon, POV, tense, and facts.
- Must not invent new story content to create false intensity.

## Mandatory Conditions
Every candidate must:
- Be copy-paste ready manuscript prose.
- Preserve POV, tense, voice, chronology, character knowledge, and canon.
- Address the identified criterion and revision objective.
- Fit before/after context.
- Avoid generic literary filler.

## Forbidden Output
Candidates must not include:
- Advice, explanation, analysis, or commentary.
- Placeholder text.
- Generic fallback prose.
- New facts, numbers, dates, locations, characters, relationships, or dialogue not supported by context.
- Model-branded or formulaic emotional beats.

## Admission Threshold
At least two of three candidates must pass quality gates before a card can be admitted to the user-facing queue.
