# DREAM Symbol-to-Character Payoff Ledger

## Purpose

The Symbol-to-Character Payoff Ledger ensures that a DREAM long-form report traces symbolic objects, names, images, places, and recurring motifs through their lifecycle rather than merely listing them.

A symbol is not covered just because it appears in a symbolic audit. It is covered only when the report explains who it attaches to, how it changes, and what payoff obligation it creates or satisfies.

## Required when

Required for every DREAM long-form report.

## Folded report surfaces

This ledger should be rendered through existing DREAM surfaces, especially:

- `symbolic_audit.preserved_symbols`
- `cross_layer_integration`
- `reader_experience`
- `revision_plan`
- `acceptance_checks.required_detection`
- `acceptance_checks.failure_conditions`

## Minimum row contract

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Symbol / object / name | Concrete recurring object, name, image, ritual, phrase, sound cue, place, map, garment, animal sign, or sensory motif. |
| Attached character(s) | Character, relationship, group, or institution the symbol belongs to or passes through. |
| First appearance | First major use or setup. |
| Transformation / transfer | Disappearance, exchange, renaming, damage, inheritance, repetition, inversion, or changed meaning. |
| Reappearance / payoff | Later appearance, closure function, reversal, aftercare use, or absence that matters. |
| Function | Identity, memory, authority, punishment, love, belonging, contamination, proof, witness, grief, or market differentiator. |
| Status | Resolved, active, underused, abandoned, overexplained, or needs manual verification. |
| Asset-protection note | What revision must preserve. |

## Hard-fail conditions

A DREAM report is incomplete if:

- It lists a major symbol but does not trace its lifecycle.
- It discusses closure while omitting a symbol's ending payoff.
- It treats a symbolic object tied to identity, naming, memory, authority, or family as atmosphere.
- It misses the transfer of a symbol between characters when that transfer alters meaning.
- It recommends revision that could damage a symbolic payoff without warning.

## Sensory-symbol rule

Sound, music, light, touch, silence, smell, taste, and repeated bodily sensations can be symbols when they create obligation, punishment, memory, intimacy, or closure. If they recur as a system, they must be considered for this ledger as well as the Sensory / Emotional Register Ledger.

## Benchmark obligations

### Froggin Noggin

Must trace Gorf, toadstone, shard, changeling/transference logic, contamination signs, amphibian doctrine objects, and human/frog mirror symbols where structurally material.

### Cartel Babies

Must trace the blue evil-eye charm lifecycle, key ring, naming/renaming, canvas/crate/bench systems, table tennis if it becomes symbolically relational, and music cues when they become punishment or control signals.

### Let the River Decide

Must trace the river, NV115, missing-person signs, dogs' refusal/sensory alerts, maps, stones, smoke/fish/camp practices, and the river-as-witness motif where materially recurring.

## Implementation note

Symbol rows must be capped and ranked by payoff weight. Prefer 5–12 strong lifecycle rows over a decorative inventory of every repeated image.
