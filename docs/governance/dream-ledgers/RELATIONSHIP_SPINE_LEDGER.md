# DREAM Relationship Spine Ledger

## Purpose

The Relationship Spine Ledger ensures that a DREAM long-form report accounts for the manuscript's load-bearing relationships, not only individual characters.

A relationship may carry more emotional, causal, thematic, or closure weight than any single character row. DREAM must identify those engines and trace their beginning pressure, midpoint shift, and ending payoff.

## Required when

Required for every DREAM long-form report.

## Folded report surfaces

This ledger should be rendered through existing DREAM surfaces, especially:

- `structural_stack`
- `cross_layer_integration`
- `reader_experience`
- `revision_plan`
- `acceptance_checks.required_detection`
- `acceptance_checks.failure_conditions`

## Minimum row contract

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Relationship label | Named dyad, triad, family unit, companion unit, antagonist pair, community relation, or collective relation. |
| Participants | Characters, groups, animals, institutions, or forces connected by the relationship. |
| Beginning pressure | Initial imbalance, distance, threat, dependency, secrecy, hierarchy, or desire. |
| Midpoint shift | Change in trust, authority, intimacy, power, fear, obligation, or recognition. |
| Ending state | Payoff, rupture, transformation, unresolved state, or underpayment. |
| Bridge mechanism | Activity, game, food, music, ritual, labor, travel, caregiving, object, place, or sensory practice that mediates the relationship. |
| Power relation | Authority, captivity, kinship, caretaking, exploitation, rivalry, social protocol, or community governance affected. |
| Trust transfer | How permission, access, loyalty, care, fear, or responsibility moves. |
| Report status | Adequately accounted for, underweighted, omitted, or needs manual verification. |

## Required bridge detection

DREAM must identify bridge activities when they materially connect hostile or unequal groups. Examples include:

- games and sports
- shared labor
- caregiving routines
- meals and food practices
- songs, music channels, or sound rituals
- animal companionship
- local cultural anchors
- travel practices
- lessons, training, or demonstrations

These are not automatically decorative. They may be the mechanism by which captives become legible to guards, children mediate adult authority, companions protect travelers, or communities transmit protocol.

## Hard-fail conditions

A DREAM report is incomplete if:

- It discusses closure but omits the dominant relationship engine.
- It treats a co-protagonist relationship as subplot when it carries the emotional spine.
- It misses a bridge activity that changes access, protection, trust, hierarchy, or ending payoff.
- It covers individual characters but fails to account for the dyad or family unit through which the reader experiences the story.
- It misses an animal/companion relationship that materially shapes survival, perception, or closure.

### Froggin Noggin

Must account for Rana/Newton as tenderness and cross-species cooperation engine; Hyla/Zimeon as authority/curiosity pressure; Chey/human damage as conscience/corruption mirror.

### Cartel Babies

Must account for Michael/Benjamin, Michael/Paolito-Paul, Raúl/Navarro, Raúl/Paolito, Benjamin/Paul, and table tennis as a bridge between captives, guards, Raúl, and Paolito.

### Let the River Decide

Must account for Mike/Cliff/dogs as travel-family unit, Smokehouse Camp protocol relationships, and the witness/community boundary between outsider narration and Indigenous story custody.

## Implementation note

The ledger must be compact and deduplicated. It should prioritize relationships that change plot access, trust, authority, reader attachment, or ending payoff.
