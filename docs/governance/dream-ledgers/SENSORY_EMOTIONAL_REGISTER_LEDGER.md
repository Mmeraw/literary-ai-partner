# DREAM Sensory / Emotional Register Ledger

## Purpose

The Sensory / Emotional Register Ledger ensures that a DREAM long-form report identifies the sensory systems that produce emotional effect on the page.

A report must not praise tone, atmosphere, trauma, dread, intimacy, place, punishment, or aftercare without accounting for the sensory channels that create those effects.

## Required when

Required for every DREAM long-form report.

## Folded report surfaces

This ledger should be rendered through existing DREAM surfaces, especially:

- `cross_layer_integration`
- `symbolic_audit`
- `reader_experience`
- `criterion_analyses` for voice, prose, tone, worldbuilding, scene construction, and closure
- `revision_plan`
- `acceptance_checks`

## Top-three sensory driver rule

The report must identify the top three sensory drivers shaping the manuscript's emotional register.

For each top driver, the report should state:

| Field | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Sense channel | Sound, touch, sight, smell, or taste. |
| Dominant anchors | Recurring concrete sensory elements. |
| Emotional register | Dread, tenderness, punishment, conditioning, relief, aftershock, belonging, disgust, disorientation, awe, etc. |
| Plot / power function | How the sense organizes control, movement, knowledge, punishment, permission, orientation, or survival. |
| Relationship function | How the sense bonds, divides, warns, mediates, or changes trust between characters. |
| Revision opportunity | What to strengthen, preserve, compress, or avoid flattening. |

## Five-senses opportunity matrix

The report must account for all five senses as one of:

- active and load-bearing
- present but underused
- intentionally restrained
- not load-bearing
- needs manual verification

## Sound / music rule

Sound and music must be detected when they function as:

- punishment or conditioning
- authority or control
- trauma trigger
- camp / community rhythm
- relationship bridge
- cultural anchor
- symbolic payoff
- silence / aftershock

A report is incomplete if it discusses tone, trauma, punishment, or governance while omitting recurring sound/music systems that materially shape fear, obedience, memory, or character bonding.

## Tactile rule

Touch and bodily sensation must be detected when they carry:

- confinement
- violence
- tenderness
- body-memory
- survival intelligence
- aftercare
- object attachment
- trust transfer

## Hard-fail conditions

A DREAM report is incomplete if:

- It treats a recurring sensory punishment/control system as atmosphere.
- It praises atmosphere without identifying the dominant sensory channels.
- It misses the sensory mechanism behind a major emotional register.
- It recommends smoothing prose in a way that would damage tactile, sonic, visual, olfactory, or taste-based architecture.

## Benchmark obligations

### Froggin Noggin

Must account for water/slime/poison/chemical contamination, amphibian body logic, grotesque human sensory comedy, song/satire where structurally material, and ecological body horror.

### Cartel Babies

Must account for music channels, Bailar, Beethoven's Fifth, sound as punishment/conditioning, canvas/crates/benches/tailgate/key tactile systems, table tennis as tactile/social bridge, and sensory aftercare in Vancouver where evidence supports them.

### Let the River Decide

Must account for river sound/silence, dogs' sensory alerts, smoke/fish/bannock/camp tactile and olfactory texture, weather, water, road fatigue, and the sensory difference between witnessing and explaining.

## Implementation note

The report should not produce a long sensory essay. It should surface the top three sensory drivers plus a concise five-senses opportunity matrix or equivalent folded findings.
